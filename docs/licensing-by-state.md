## Overview

Every U.S. business requires some combination of federal, state, county,
and/or municipal licenses and permits. There is no single federal business
license that covers all activity — the requirements depend entirely on:
- **What your business does** (industry/activity)
- **Where your business is located** (state, county, city)

> "Most small businesses need a combination of licenses and permits from
> both federal and state agencies. The requirements and fees vary based
> on your business activities, location, and government rules."
> — SBA.gov (sba.gov/business-guide/launch-your-business/apply-licenses-permits)

This document provides a framework for understanding licensing requirements
by category and identifies state-specific considerations most relevant to
Canadian E-2 investors.

## Part 1 — Federal Licenses

Federal licenses are required only when your business activity is regulated
by a **federal agency.** Most small businesses do NOT need a federal license.

The following industries require federal licenses:

| Business Activity | Federal Agency |
|---|---|
| Agriculture (import/transport of animals, biologics, plants across state lines) | U.S. Dept. of Agriculture |
| Alcoholic beverages (manufacture, wholesale, import, retail sale) | Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) + Local ABC Board |
| Aviation (aircraft operation, cargo, maintenance) | Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) |
| Firearms, ammunition, explosives (manufacture, sale, import) | Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) |
| Fish and wildlife (any wildlife-related activity, import/export) | U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service |
| Commercial fisheries | NOAA Fisheries Service |
| Maritime transportation | Federal Maritime Commission |
| Mining and drilling (on federal lands) | Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement |
| Nuclear energy | U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission |
| Radio and television broadcasting | Federal Communications Commission (FCC) |
| Transportation and logistics (oversize vehicles) | U.S. Dept. of Transportation |

> Source: SBA.gov — Apply for Licenses and Permits (2026)

**For most E-2 franchise and service businesses:** Federal licensing is
not required. The federal license categories above affect specialized
industries uncommon in typical E-2 applications.

## Part 2 — State Licenses and Permits

State licensing is where most E-2 investors spend the majority of their
compliance effort. State requirements vary significantly by industry and
by state.

### Universal State Requirements (Nearly Every Business)

**Business entity registration:**
- Every LLC or corporation must register with the Secretary of State (or
  equivalent agency) in the state where it operates
- This is separate from federal EIN registration
- Annual report / renewal fee required in most states

**State business license (general):**
- Some states require a general state business license in addition to
  industry-specific licenses
- Examples: Washington State Business License, Nevada Business License,
  Georgia Business License
- Not all states require a general state-level license — confirm for
  your specific state

**State tax registration:**
- Sales tax permit (if you sell taxable goods or services) — required
  in states with a sales tax
- Employer withholding registration (if you have employees)
- Unemployment insurance registration

### Industry-Specific State Licensing — Common E-2 Categories

**Food Service / Restaurant:**
- State food service handler's permit or food establishment license
- Issued by state Department of Health or Department of Agriculture
- Requires food safety inspection before opening
- Manager ServSafe certification required in most states
- Separate county/city health department permit typically required
- Liquor license (state ABC board) — if serving alcohol; one of the most
  complex and time-consuming licensing processes (can take 3–6+ months)
- Fees: $100–$1,500+ depending on state and license type
- Key states: California (CDPH + ABC), Texas (DSHS + TABC), Florida (DBPR),
  New York (DOH + SLA)

**Home Services (Cleaning, Landscaping, Painting):**
- Contractor's license required in many states for any work over a
  de minimis threshold
- California: Contractor's State License Board (CSLB) — one of the most
  complex; requires trade exam in some categories
- Florida: DBPR — various contractor license classifications
- Texas: Generally less regulated at state level; licensing varies by
  municipality
- General cleaning services typically do NOT require a contractor's license
- Pesticide application: EPA certification required; state-level
  pesticide applicator license required in all states

**Childcare Services:**
- State childcare licensing is required in all 50 states
- Issued by state Department of Children and Family Services or equivalent
- Background checks required for all staff
- Facility inspection required
- Staff-to-child ratios mandated
- Lead time for licensing: 3–6 months before opening is typical
- This is one of the most regulated industries — begin licensing early

**Senior Care / Home Health:**
- Home Health Agency (HHA) license required in most states
- Some states require Medicare/Medicaid certification even for private-pay services
- Complex regulatory environment — varies dramatically by state
- California, New York, and New Jersey: most restrictive
- Texas and Florida: moderate regulation
- Some states have moratoriums on new HHA licenses (research your target state)

**Automotive Services:**
- Auto repair / service facility: most states require a dealer or
  repair facility license
- Arizona: ROC (Registrar of Contractors) for some categories
- California: Bureau of Automotive Repair (BAR) license required
- New York: Department of Motor Vehicles facility registration
- Emissions inspection stations: state-specific certification program

**Fitness / Gym:**
- State health club or fitness center registration in some states
  (consumer protection statutes governing membership contracts)
- California: Health Studio Services Contract Law
- Florida: Chapter 501, Florida Statutes — prepaid entertainment contracts
- General building and occupancy permits required everywhere
- Personal trainer: state-level certification not universally required,
  but national certifications (NASM, ACE) expected by consumers

**Staffing / Temporary Employment Agencies:**
- Temporary help agency license required in several states
- California, Illinois, New Jersey: explicit licensing requirements
- Most states: employer-of-record registration and workers' comp insurance

**Real Estate (if your franchise involves property management):**
- Real estate broker/salesperson license required for property management
  in most states — E-2 investors who are not licensed cannot perform
  licensed activities themselves
- Consider hiring a licensed property manager

## Part 3 — County and Municipal Permits

In addition to federal and state requirements, most businesses need:

**Local business license / business tax certificate:**
- Required by most incorporated cities and counties
- Often called a Business Tax Certificate (CA), Business License (general),
  or Certificate of Occupancy
- Fees: $50–$500+ per year depending on municipality
- Separate from state registration — you often need both

**Zoning / land use approval:**
- Confirm the location is zoned for your type of business before signing a lease
- Use permit or conditional use permit may be required for certain
  business types in residential-adjacent zones
- Home-based businesses have additional restrictions in most jurisdictions

**Building permits:**
- Required for any physical build-out or improvements to leased space
- Tenant improvement (TI) permits issued by local building department
- Franchise build-outs typically require permits

**Health department permits:**
- Required for any food service, childcare, or healthcare-adjacent business
- Issued by county health department
- Inspection required before opening

**Signage permits:**
- Required in most jurisdictions for exterior signage
- Franchise branding signs require compliance with both the franchise
  agreement and local sign codes

**Fire Marshal approval:**
- Required before opening for most commercial businesses
- Occupancy load determination, emergency exits, fire suppression systems

## Part 4 — Professional Licenses (Operator Level)

Some businesses require the owner/operator to hold a professional license
in addition to the business entity license.

**Important for E-2 applicants:** You cannot transfer a Canadian professional
license to the United States. Each state issues its own professional
licenses. Some states have reciprocity agreements, but these generally
apply to U.S. citizens or permanent residents moving between states —
not to foreign nationals.

| Profession | Licensing Body | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General Contractor | State licensing board | Varies enormously by state |
| Electrical Contractor | State/local board | Exam required in most states |
| Plumber | State/local board | Exam required; journeyman/master levels |
| HVAC | EPA 608 (federal) + state | EPA certification for refrigerants is federal |
| Real Estate Broker | State real estate commission | Exam required; residency rules vary |
| Insurance Agent | State insurance department | Exam required; non-resident licensing available in most states |
| CPA / Accountant | State board of accountancy | CPA exam; typically requires U.S. residency |
| Cosmetologist / Barber | State cosmetology board | Exam + hours; Canadian hours may not transfer |
| Childcare Director | State DCFS | Training + background check |

**For most E-2 franchise investors:** You do not need to personally hold
the professional license if you hire licensed staff. However, you must
ensure your business is properly licensed and that licensed employees
hold required credentials.

## Part 5 — Licensing Timeline for E-2 Planning Purposes

| License Type | Typical Lead Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| State entity registration | 1–3 days (online) | Do this first |
| EIN (federal) | Same day | Online at IRS.gov/EIN |
| General local business license | 1–4 weeks | Some jurisdictions are faster |
| State food service license | 2–8 weeks | Requires inspection |
| Liquor license | 3–6+ months | Start immediately; varies by state/county |
| Childcare license | 3–6 months | Facility inspection + staff checks |
| Home health agency license | 3–12 months | Highly variable by state |
| Contractor's license (CA) | 3–6 months | Exam-based |
| Building/TI permits | 2–8 weeks after application | Varies by jurisdiction |
| Certificate of Occupancy | After TI inspection | After build-out completion |

**Practical planning rule:** Identify every required license on Day 1 of
business planning. Long-lead licenses (liquor, childcare, home health)
must begin the application process months before your target opening date.
Missing licenses delay your opening, which delays your E-2 application
evidence and your first-year revenue.

## Part 6 — How to Research Your Specific Requirements

1. **SBA License & Permit Tool:** sba.gov — enter your ZIP code and
   business type for a state/local checklist
2. **State Secretary of State website:** Business registration requirements
3. **State Department of Revenue / Taxation:** Sales tax and employer registration
4. **County Assessor / Business License office:** Local business license
5. **State industry-specific board:** Health, contractor, real estate, etc.
6. **City/County Planning Department:** Zoning verification before signing a lease
7. **Your franchise's FDD Operations Manual:** Most franchise systems list
   required licenses in their operations manuals — follow this as a baseline
