## Overview

E-2 visa status can be renewed indefinitely as long as the business remains
active and the investor continues to satisfy all E-2 requirements. There is
no maximum number of renewals and no statutory limit on total time in E-2
status.

> "The E-2 visa status can be extended in perpetuity for as long as the
> business is in operation in the United States."
> — Ford Murray Law (referencing INA 101(a)(15)(E))

However, each renewal is a **fresh adjudication** — the officer evaluates
your current application against the current standards. Prior approval
does not guarantee renewal.

> "E-2 visa holders seeking renewals should be aware that consular officers
> are likely to reassess the enterprise's viability at each renewal stage."
> — Stelmakh Law — E-2 Visa Processing Trends in 2026

## Two Renewal Paths

### Path 1 — Consular Renewal (New Visa Stamp)

Renewing at a U.S. consulate abroad. For Canadians, this typically means:
- **U.S. Consulate General in Montreal** (handles most Canadian E-2 applications)
- You travel to Canada (or remain in Canada) and attend a new interview

**When required:**
- Your current visa stamp has expired
- You need a new visa stamp to re-enter the United States
- Your passport has expired and you need a new visa in your new passport
- You had a change of employer or business that requires consular review

**What you submit:**
- New complete E-2 application package (DS-160, DS-156E)
- Updated supporting documentation reflecting current business status
- Updated business plan (actual vs. projected comparison)
- Current financial statements (P&L, balance sheet)
- Updated hiring plan / current employee roster with payroll records
- Updated source of funds (if any additional investment was made)
- Current business bank account statements (last 6–12 months)
- State and federal tax returns for operating years
- Business licenses and permits (current)
- Lease agreement (current or renewed)

**Processing times (2026):**
> "Recent data show consular processing at approximately 3 months, subject
> to vary."
> — Tryalma — E-2 Processing Time 2026

At high-volume posts, wait times for interview appointments can range from
several weeks to several months, with administrative processing potentially
adding further time.
> Source: Stelmakh Law — E-2 Visa Processing Trends in 2026

**Note for Canadians:** Canadians do not require a visa to enter the
United States as visitors — but E-2 status requires the visa stamp to
be current. Canadian E-2 holders who let their visa stamp expire while
remaining in the U.S. can apply for an extension of status through USCIS
(see Path 2) or must travel to Canada to renew at the consulate.

### Path 2 — USCIS Extension of Status (I-129)

Extending E-2 status inside the United States without leaving. The investor
remains in the U.S. and files **Form I-129 (Petition for Nonimmigrant Worker)**
with USCIS.

**When available:**
- You are currently in valid E-2 status (status has not expired)
- You want to extend your authorized period of stay without traveling abroad
- Note: This extends your **status** (permission to remain in the U.S.)
  but does NOT give you a new **visa stamp**
  — if you leave the U.S. after filing I-129 but before a consular visa,
  you must obtain a new consular visa before re-entering

**Processing times (2026):**
> "USCIS processing for E-2 extensions is approximately 2–3 months for
> standard processing (based on FY 2024 medians)."
> — Tryalma — E-2 Processing Time 2026

**Premium Processing:**
Form I-129 E-2 extensions are eligible for USCIS Premium Processing
(Form I-907), which guarantees a 15-business-day adjudication for an
additional fee (currently ~$2,805 as of 2025–2026 — confirm current
fees at uscis.gov). Widely used by investors who need certainty of timing.

**What you submit with I-129:**
- Form I-129 with E classification supplement
- Evidence of continued E-2 eligibility (same categories as consular renewal)
- I-94 showing current valid status
- Support letter from the E-2 business

## What Changes at Renewal — The Critical Difference from Initial Application

At the initial application, the officer evaluates your **plan** — projections,
intended hiring, anticipated revenue.

At renewal, the officer evaluates your **actual performance** compared
to those projections. This is the most significant difference.

### Documents Unique to Renewal (Not in Initial Application)

**Tax returns:**
- U.S. federal tax returns (Form 1040 with Schedule C, or Form 1120-S)
  for all operating years
- State business tax returns (where applicable)

**Business financial statements:**
- Profit and loss statement (income statement) for all operating periods
- Balance sheet
- If the business has a CPA: compiled or reviewed financial statements
  are more credible than owner-prepared

**Bank statements:**
- Business bank account statements for 12 months before the renewal date
- Personal bank account statements showing owner distributions or salary

**Payroll records:**
- Payroll reports showing all employees, hours, and wages paid
- IRS Form 941 filings (quarterly payroll tax deposits)
- IRS W-2 records for all employees

**State tax filings:**
- Sales tax returns
- Employer payroll tax state filings

**Business licenses:**
- Current licenses (showing no lapses or violations)

## Actual vs. Projected — The Template 7 Analysis

The most important document for renewal — one that E2Pathway generates as
Template 7 — is the **Actual vs. Projected Comparison.**

This is a side-by-side comparison showing:

| Metric | Original Projection | Actual Results | Variance | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 Revenue | $250,000 | $198,000 | -21% | Extended build-out delayed opening by 2 months |
| Year 1 Net Income | $45,000 | $28,000 | -38% | Higher than projected initial staffing costs |
| Year 1 Employees | 3 FT, 2 PT | 2 FT, 2 PT | -1 FT | Delayed hiring due to slower ramp-up |
| Year 2 Revenue (projected) | $320,000 | — | — | — |

**Why this document is essential:**
Officers scrutinize variance between projections and actuals. Without a
proactive explanation, an officer who sees revenue 20% below projection may
conclude the business is struggling and approaching marginal status.

A well-prepared renewal explains every material variance with a business
reason (supply chain delays, opening delays, staffing challenges, market
conditions) and demonstrates that the business is on track to meet or
exceed projections over the 5-year horizon.

**If the business has significantly underperformed:**
- Address it honestly — do not paper over it
- Provide a credible recovery plan with specific milestones
- Show trend improvement (month-by-month trajectory)
- Consult your immigration attorney before filing — significant
  underperformance is a renewal risk that should be evaluated professionally

## Renewal Timeline — When to Start

| Trigger | Recommended Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 6 months before visa stamp expires | Begin renewal preparation | Gather financial documents, tax returns, updated business plan |
| 5 months before | Engage immigration attorney if needed | Attorney review of actual vs. projected variance |
| 4 months before | File I-129 (USCIS path) OR schedule consular appointment | Premium processing if timing-sensitive |
| 3 months before | Interview or I-129 adjudication | Standard timeline |
| Visa stamp expired, status valid | File I-129 immediately | Do not delay — maintain valid status |
| Status expired | Depart U.S. immediately; apply consularly | Overstay is a serious violation |

## E-2 Dependent Renewals

Dependants (spouse and unmarried children under 21) renew simultaneously
with or following the principal investor's renewal.

- Dependants renew on the same I-129 filing or consular application
  as the principal investor
- E-2D dependent spouse's EAD must be separately renewed (Form I-765)
- Children approaching 21 require separate planning — see `kb/dependent-age-out.md`

## Registering the E-2 Company at Renewal

The U.S. Embassy Canada requires that the E-2 company maintain its
registered status with the consulate:

> "First-time E-1/E-2 (Treaty Trader/Investor) visa applicants and those
> wishing to renew the registration status of their E-visa company must
> apply at the U.S. Embassy or Consulate."
> — U.S. Embassy Canada — Treaty Trader and Investor Visas FAQs

Company registration renewal ensures the consulate's records reflect
current ownership, business address, and operational status.
