## U.S. Embassy London (United Kingdom)

### Section 1 — Application Structure
- **Submission:** Digital upload via U.S. Embassy London online portal — NOT physical binder
- **File size limit:** Maximum **20MB** total 🗓️
- **Documents:** DS-160, MRV fee receipt, passport photos, passport copy, business plan, investment evidence, source of funds, org documents, lease/purchase, cover letter
- **Additional:** Companies House extract (UK business registration); 12 months bank statements
- **Language:** English only — no translation required
- **Cover letter:** Addressed to "Nonimmigrant Visa Unit, U.S. Embassy London"; summarize treaty basis, investment, business, job creation

### Section 2 — Interview Process
- **Required:** Yes — U.S. Embassy, 33 Nine Elms Lane, London SW11 7US
- **Duration:** 10–20 minutes 🔄
- **Tone:** Professional and businesslike; less adversarial than some posts (community reported)
- **Common questions:** Describe your business; how did you fund the investment; how many employees will you hire; what is your day-to-day role; where is the business located; what is your exit plan
- **Attorneys:** May accompany to embassy; generally NOT permitted inside interview room 🔄 Verify
- **Interpreters:** Not required for UK nationals

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** 🔄 2–4 months (verify via ustraveldocs.com)
- **Interview to issuance:** 5–10 business days if approved without admin processing
- **Expedited:** Available — limited criteria; contact embassy directly
- **Validity issued:** Typically **5 years** for UK nationals 🗓️
- **Status on entry:** Up to 2 years per admission; renewable

### Section 4 — Known Consulate-Specific Requirements
- **20MB cap is a hard London limit** — compress large PDFs before submission 🗓️
- Cover letters should be concise and structured — verbose applications not favored
- Business plan must demonstrate genuine job creation for U.S. workers
- Franchise applications: FDD (Franchise Disclosure Document) expected
- Foreign-sourced funds may require certified translation; UK-sourced docs do not require apostille

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds
- No official minimum; informal threshold **$100,000+**
- Investments below $80,000 receive heavy scrutiny (community reported)
- Service and consulting businesses scrutinized for "marginality"
- Investment must be "at risk" — escrow arrangements accepted if properly structured

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns
- **Most common:** Marginality; insufficient investment; no genuine managerial role
- **221(g):** Not uncommon for first-time applicants; often requests additional financial docs or business plan revisions 🔄
- **High-scrutiny types:** Solo consulting/coaching; online-only businesses; very low startup cost businesses

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal / ustraveldocs.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD 🗓️
- **Biometrics:** Collected at embassy on interview day
- **DS-160:** Select "London"; barcode must match appointment record
- **Backlogs:** Moderate — 2–4 months typical 🗓️

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- Well-developed E-2 immigration attorney community in London
- Strong franchise broker network; U.S. franchise brands actively market E-2 to UK nationals
- E-2 is well understood in the UK — prominently marketed at immigration and franchise expos
- **Cultural note:** Emphasize "hands-on management" role to distinguish from passive investment

## U.S. Consulate Toronto (Canada)

- **Submission:** Physical organized tabbed binder brought to interview 🔄 Verify if digital intake added

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** ~**4 months** as of Q4 2025 (Frear Law verified attorney data) 🗓️
- **Interview to issuance:** 7–10 business days; longer if admin processing triggered
- **Expedited:** Available — apply through ustraveldocs.com; requires documented urgent business need
- **Validity issued:** Typically **5 years** for Canadian nationals 🗓️
- **Status on entry:** Up to 2 years per admission; renewable

### Section 4 — Known Consulate-Specific Requirements

- Tabbed physical binder is expected standard format
- Cover letter should address CUSMA/USMCA treaty basis explicitly
- Financial projections: 3–5 year pro forma required; must show path beyond marginal income
- 🔄 Verify if digital upload option introduced since Q4 2025

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns

- **221(g):** Requests for updated business plan; proof of active business operations; additional financial records
- **High-scrutiny:** Online businesses; real estate investment (unless substantial active management); single-employee operations

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal via ustraveldocs.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD 🗓️
- **Biometrics:** Collected at consulate on interview day
- **DS-160:** Select "Toronto"; barcode must match appointment

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- Strong E-2 attorney community in Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary
- Franchise brokers in Canada explicitly market E-2 pathways; very active market
- E-2 well understood and frequently discussed in Canadian immigrant entrepreneur communities
- **Cultural note:** Strong business documentation habits; emphasize active U.S.-based management role
