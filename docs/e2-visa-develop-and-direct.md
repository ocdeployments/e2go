# E-2 Visa — Develop and Direct Requirement
**kb/e2-visa-develop-and-direct.md**
*Sources: 9 FAM 402.9-6(F) — State Department Foreign Affairs Manual (fam.state.gov);
22 CFR 41.51(a)(2); INA 101(a)(15)(E)(ii); RJ Immigration Law — Developing and Directing*
*Last reviewed: May 2026*

---

## The Legal Requirement

The E-2 investor must be coming to the United States to **develop and direct**
the enterprise. This is not just a formality — it is an independent eligibility
requirement evaluated by consular officers.

> "The treaty investor must be coming to the U.S. to develop and direct the
> investment enterprise. The treaty investor must demonstrate this by showing
> at least 50% ownership of the enterprise or possession of operational
> control through a managerial position or other corporate device."
> — 9 FAM 402.9-6(F)

---

## Two Ways to Satisfy Develop and Direct

### Method 1 — 50% or More Ownership

If you own **50% or more** of the qualifying enterprise, you presumptively
satisfy the develop and direct requirement. Your ownership stake is evidence
that you have the ability to control and direct the business.

**Acceptable ownership structures:**
- 100% sole ownership
- 50/50 co-ownership with one other person (each applicant independently
  satisfies the 50% threshold for their own application)
- 51%+ ownership in a multi-investor structure

**Documentation:**
- Articles of incorporation or organization showing ownership percentages
- Operating agreement (for LLC) detailing ownership interests
- Stock register (for a corporation)
- Franchise agreement identifying you as the franchisee/owner

### Method 2 — Operational Control Through a Managerial Position

If you own less than 50%, you can still satisfy the requirement if you
possess **operational control** through a managerial position or other
corporate device (e.g., board seat, contractual management authority).

> "The treaty investor who cannot show at least 50% ownership must demonstrate
> possession of operational control through a managerial position or some
> other corporate device."
> — 9 FAM 402.9-6(F)

In practice, this pathway is uncommon for initial E-2 applications — most
applicants either own 100% or 50% of the business.

## What "Develop and Direct" Means in Practice

The officer evaluates whether you will be **actively managing the business**
— not merely a passive investor who hired a manager to run it on your behalf.

### Active Management — What It Looks Like

You are expected to:
- Set and implement business strategy and objectives
- Make hiring and personnel decisions
- Manage day-to-day operations (or manage those who manage day-to-day)
- Be physically present at the business location (for most business types)
- Have authority over financial decisions
- Be the primary decision-maker for the enterprise

### The Executive/Manager vs. Employee Distinction

E-2 principal investors are approved as **executives or managers** — not as
employees performing operational tasks.

> "If the treaty investor's only role is to perform operational duties, the
> develop and direct requirement may not be met."
> — 9 FAM 402.9-6(F)

**This does not mean you cannot do any hands-on work.** A franchise owner
who sometimes makes coffee, handles a register, or performs tasks alongside
employees can still satisfy develop and direct — as long as their **primary
role is managerial** and they have actual decision-making authority.

What matters is:
- Your job title is executive or managerial (e.g., Owner/Operator, President,
  Managing Director, General Manager)
- Your described duties are primarily strategic and managerial
- You have authority to hire, fire, set strategy, and control finances

### Passive Investment — What Fails Develop and Direct

You will fail the develop and direct test if:
- You are simply an investor who provided capital and hired a full-time
  manager to run all operations
- You have no defined role in the business beyond ownership
- You will not be physically present in the U.S. to operate the business
- Another person (a local manager) makes all material business decisions
  and you merely receive distributions

## The 50/50 Partnership — Special Considerations

When two people co-invest at 50/50, each person independently satisfies
the 50% ownership threshold and can each apply for E-2 status.

**The critical issue:** Each applicant must independently satisfy develop
and direct — meaning each person must have a genuine, differentiated
managerial role in the business.

Officers will examine:
- How are the two applicants' management responsibilities divided?
- Does each person have a defined area of the business they lead?
- Is there meaningful role differentiation, or are both people claiming
  the same job?

**Strong role differentiation example:**
- Partner A: COO — operations, staffing, day-to-day management, vendor
  relations, customer service
- Partner B: CFO/CMO — financial management, marketing, business development,
  strategic partnerships

**Weak role differentiation (raises questions):**
- Both applicants: "General management and oversight of all operations"

See `kb/e2-visa-partnership-applications.md` for full partnership guidance.

## Your Role in the Cover Letter and Business Plan

The develop and direct requirement must be explicitly addressed in:

**Cover Letter (Paragraph 2):**
State your ownership percentage and describe your specific role and
management responsibilities in the business. Use the job title that appears
in the business plan's organizational chart.

**Business Plan:**
- Organizational chart showing your position at the top
- Narrative description of your management role
- Your specific decision-making authorities
- How you will be involved in daily operations

**DS-160:**
Your occupation and employer information must be consistent with an executive
or managerial role. Do not describe yourself as a regular employee.

## Remote Management — Does It Satisfy Develop and Direct?

The E-2 visa requires the investor to **come to the United States** to
develop and direct the enterprise. A fully remote ownership arrangement —
where you remain in Canada and manage a U.S. business by video call —
does not satisfy the E-2 requirements.

You must be physically present in the United States to operate the business.
This is consistent with the visa's fundamental purpose: to bring the
investor to the U.S. to operate the enterprise.

Occasional travel outside the U.S. for business or personal reasons is
permitted and normal. What is not permitted is using E-2 status as an
authorization to operate a U.S. business while residing primarily abroad.

## Proving Develop and Direct — Document Checklist

| Document | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| LLC Operating Agreement or Corporate Bylaws | Ownership percentage and management authority |
| Franchise Agreement | You as the owner-operator of the franchise |
| Business Plan — Management Section | Your specific role, responsibilities, and authority |
| Organizational Chart | Your position at the top of the hierarchy |
| Cover Letter — Management Paragraph | Your explicit description of your active management role |
| Employment Contract (if applicable) | Your title, responsibilities, and compensation |
| Professional Biography / CV | Your background that qualifies you to manage this business |
