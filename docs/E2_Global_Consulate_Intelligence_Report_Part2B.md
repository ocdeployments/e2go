## U.S. Embassy Buenos Aires (Argentina)

### Section 1 — Application Structure
- **Submission:** CGI Federal; physical package at interview 🔄 Verify
- **Documents:** Standard E-2 package; Argentine corporate docs (estatuto social, IGJ inscription) with certified English translation
- **Language:** All Spanish documents require certified English translation
- **Additional:** AFIP tax records; bank statements; lawful source of funds proof

### Section 2 — Interview Process
- **Required:** Yes — U.S. Embassy Buenos Aires, Av. Colombia 4300
- **Duration:** 10–20 minutes 🔄
- **Common questions:** Nature of U.S. business; source of investment funds; management role; hiring plan; industry experience
- **Interpreters:** Spanish-speaking staff at embassy

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** 🔄 2–5 months
- **Validity issued:** Typically **5 years** 🗓️

### Section 4 — Known Requirements
- Argentina has significant currency volatility — source of funds must clearly establish USD-equivalent value at time of investment
- IGJ corporate registration extract required for Argentine-entity investments
- AFIP tax records for source of funds

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds
- Informal threshold: **$100,000+**
- Currency conversion documentation critical given Argentine peso volatility

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns
- **Most common:** Source of funds gaps; marginality; passive investment
- **221(g):** Financial document review 🔄
- **High-scrutiny:** Cash-funded investments without clear paper trail

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal / ustraveldocs.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD 🗓️ | **DS-160:** Select "Buenos Aires"

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- Active U.S. immigration attorney community in Buenos Aires
- Food/beverage, technology, and real estate-adjacent businesses common
- **Cultural note:** Thorough source-of-funds documentation is the most critical preparation element

## U.S. Embassy Islamabad / Consulates (Pakistan)

### Section 1 — Application Structure
- **Posts:** U.S. Embassy Islamabad (primary); Consulates Karachi, Lahore, Peshawar
- **Submission:** CGI Federal via usvisa-info.com 🗓️
- **Documents:** Standard E-2 package; Pakistani corporate docs (SECP registration, Form A) with certified English translation
- **Language:** English preferred; Urdu documents require certified English translation
- **Additional:** FBR (Federal Board of Revenue) tax records; bank statements; SECP corporate filings
- **⚠️ IMPORTANT:** As of January 21, 2026, immigrant visa issuances for Pakistani nationals are paused. **E-2 nonimmigrant visa processing remains ACTIVE and unaffected.** 🗓️

### Section 2 — Interview Process
- **Required:** Yes — in person at applicable post
- **Duration:** 10–20 minutes 🔄
- **Tone:** Professional; officers experienced with Pakistani applicant profiles 🔄
- **Common questions:** Source of investment funds; business description; management role; hiring plan; Pakistan business background
- **Interpreters:** Urdu/Punjabi-speaking staff available 🔄
- **Attorneys:** 🔄 Generally not inside interview room

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** ~**7 months** per State Dept Global Visa Wait Times (2026) — one of the longer waits globally 🗓️
- **Interview to issuance:** 5–10 business days if approved
- **Admin processing:** Longer delays possible; background check admin processing reported for some Pakistani nationals 🔄
- **Validity issued:** Typically **5 years** 🔄 Verify via reciprocity schedule
- **Status on entry:** Up to 2 years per admission

### Section 4 — Known Consulate-Specific Requirements
- SECP registration for Pakistani-entity investments
- FBR tax records required for source of funds — critical documentation element
- Certified English translation for all Urdu documents
- Pakistani rupee to USD conversion clearly documented
- **Platform action:** Display clear notice — "E-2 is a nonimmigrant visa and is NOT affected by the January 2026 immigrant visa pause for Pakistan" 🗓️

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds
- Informal threshold: **$100,000+**
- Pakistani investors often target franchise, food service, and retail U.S. businesses
- Source of funds documentation is the most critical component given officer scrutiny

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns
- **Most common:** Source of funds gaps; admin processing holds; marginality
- **221(g):** Admin processing for background checks more common at Islamabad than Western European posts (community reported) 🔄
- **High-scrutiny:** Cash-funded investments; businesses without clear operational plan
- **Platform note:** Pakistani users should be advised of 7-month appointment wait AND potential admin processing delays upfront

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal via usvisa-info.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD 🗓️
- **Biometrics:** Collected at post
- **DS-160:** Select specific Pakistan post (Islamabad, Karachi, Lahore, or Peshawar)
- **Backlogs:** ~7 months per State Dept data 🗓️
- **⚠️ Monitor:** Immigrant visa pause — verify no NIV spillover monthly 🗓️

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- Growing Pakistani E-2 attorney community; many U.S.-based Pakistani immigration attorneys serve this market
- Pakistani diaspora investor networks in the U.S. actively share E-2 information
- Franchise investment is most common E-2 vehicle for Pakistani nationals
- **Cultural note:** Coach on clear, direct communication of source of funds; thorough paper trail essential; rehearse financial documentation narrative before interview
