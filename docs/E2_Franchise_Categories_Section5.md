# E-2 Pathway Platform — Franchise Categories by E-2 Strength
## Vol 3 Supplement: Section 5

**Document:** `docs/spec/E2_Franchise_Categories_Section5.md`
**Version:** 1.0 — May 2026
**Sources:** FDD disclosures, USCIS E-2 standard (INA §101(a)(15)(E)(ii)), immigration attorney publications (2025–2026), SBA/franchise industry data, State Dept E-2 adjudication criteria
**Flags:** ⚠️ = uncertain or contested E-2 compatibility | 🔄 = verify before relying on

> **Methodology note:** USCIS and U.S. consulates do not publish category-level E-2 approval rates.
> The "E-2 compatibility" assessments below are grounded in the official E-2 standard:
> the enterprise must be real, active, substantial, at-risk, and non-marginal,
> with the investor directing and developing it. Categories that naturally
> produce payroll, leased premises, and active operations fit this standard more
> cleanly than low-headcount or passive-feeling models.
> Toronto consulate patterns are practical inferences based on published attorney guidance —
> not official statistics.

---

## E-2 Strength Legend

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Strong** | Naturally fits all E-2 criteria; low risk of marginality challenge |
| **Moderate to Strong** | Usually qualifies; execution details matter |
| **Moderate** | Qualifies in right structure; weaker if undercapitalized or owner-dependent |
| ⚠️ **Mixed / Contested** | Can qualify but draws elevated scrutiny; structure is critical |
| ⚠️ **Uncertain** | Regularly contested; requires strong attorney structuring |

---

# 1. Home Care / Senior Care

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Staffing-heavy, active service model with non-marginal hiring; directly employs U.S. workers as caregivers; investor directs and develops the enterprise rather than performing the service personally |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $250,000+ (FDD Item 7 varies by brand; territory fees, working capital, and licensing costs are primary drivers) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 3–10 (caregiver payroll is core — weak cases show investor as sole worker; strong cases show W-2 caregivers hired before or at launch) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous, not required — franchise system provides care training and compliance guidance; business management background is more important |
| **Example Brands** | Visiting Angels, SYNERGY HomeCare, Right at Home, A Place at Home, Interim HealthCare, BrightSpring, Senior Helpers |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Likely favorable when file shows staffing plan, state/provincial licensure readiness, and active caregiver hiring; weaker if framed as personal caregiving by the investor |
| **Key Risk** | Under-staffed at launch; investor performing care work personally rather than managing |
| **Platform Flag** | None — recommend as Tier 1 category |

---

# 2. Child Education / Tutoring

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Moderate to Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Center-based education franchises with instructors on payroll and leased premises qualify well; solo tutoring by the investor alone risks marginality |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $250,000+ (territory fee, center buildout, curriculum licensing) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 2–8 (instructors/tutors hired as W-2 or scheduled part-time staff) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous, not usually required; education background helps credibility at interview |
| **Example Brands** | Kumon, Mathnasium, School of Rock, The Learning Experience, Tutor Doctor, Sylvan Learning |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Stronger when center has leased premises, scheduled instructors, and enrolled students; weaker when home-based or owner-dependent |
| **Key Risk** | Marginalilty challenge if the only "employee" is the investor-teacher |
| **Platform Flag** | Warn if applicant indicates home-based or no additional staff |

---

# 3. Fitness / Wellness / Yoga

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Moderate to Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Physical facility with memberships, equipment investment, and front desk/training staff clears the at-risk and non-marginal tests well; boutique studios with no payroll are weaker |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $200,000 – $975,000+ depending on brand and facility size (Anytime Fitness total startup costs $400K–$975K per FDD; boutique studios lower) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 3–10 (front desk, trainers, cleaners) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous but not required; large franchise systems provide full operator training |
| **Example Brands** | Anytime Fitness, Orangetheory Fitness, StretchLab, Club Pilates, YogaSix, F45 Training, Burn Boot Camp |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Stronger for full gym concepts with equipment spend, lease, and staff; boutique yoga draws marginality scrutiny if staffing is thin and investment is low |
| **Key Risk** | Undercapitalized boutique concepts that look like personal training practices |
| **Platform Flag** | Surface investment adequacy warning if total investment below $150K for this category |

---

# 4. Food Service / QSR / Fast Casual

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Operationally intensive, jobs-creating, tangible assets (equipment, lease, inventory); one of the clearest fits for E-2 criteria; officers at high-volume posts are experienced with this category |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $150,000 – $500,000+ (buildout and equipment are primary costs; many brands exceed this range) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 8–25 (kitchen, counter, management staff) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous but not required; franchisor training programs are extensive and well-documented |
| **Example Brands** | Subway, Wingstop, Popeyes, Jersey Mike's, The Great Greek, Chicken Salad Chick, Slim Chickens, Tropical Smoothie Café |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Consistently one of the stronger categories at Toronto; officers understand the model; risk rises only when undercapitalized or when the lease and equipment are not yet secured |
| **Key Risk** | Undercapitalization; applications filed before lease or equipment commitment |
| **Platform Flag** | None — recommend as Tier 1 category; flag if investment below $150K |

---

# 5. Cleaning / Restoration / Property Services

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Commercial cleaning and restoration are active, contract-based, and naturally staffing-driven; clearly non-passive; investor manages crews and contracts rather than personally cleaning |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $250,000+ (equipment, vehicle fleet, territory, training; some brands advertise lower entry but $100K+ is safer for E-2 presentation) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 3–12 (cleaning crews or restoration technicians as W-2 or scheduled employees) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous, not usually required; franchise training covers operations and compliance |
| **Example Brands** | The Maids, SERVPRO, Jan-Pro Cleaning & Disinfecting, Molly Maid, PuroClean, 1-800-WATER DAMAGE, Restoration 1 |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Likely favorable when file shows employee hiring plan, equipment purchase, and commercial contracts; weaker if it looks like the investor is the sole worker |
| **Key Risk** | Solo operator structure; no employees at launch |
| **Platform Flag** | Warn if no payroll planned in Year 1 |

---

# 6. ⚠️ IT Services / Tech Repair

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | ⚠️ **Mixed / Contested** |
| **Official Basis** | Active IT service or managed services businesses can qualify; however, many IT franchise models are low-asset, low-headcount, and closely resemble professional self-employment — which draws marginality challenges |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $200,000+ for credible E-2 positioning (even if FDD entry cost is lower, total investment should be padded to meet the substantiality standard) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 1–5 (the low headcount is the core risk) |
| **Industry Experience** | More important here than in most categories; an officer may question credibility if the investor has no IT background at all |
| **Example Brands** | NerdsToGo, TeamLogic IT, uBreakiFix (now Asurion Tech Repair), CMIT Solutions |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Likely cautious unless the file shows physical premises, genuine staff hiring, client contracts, and an investor with relevant background; weak if it resembles solo consulting or personal repair work |
| **Key Risk** | Marginality; single-employee or owner-operator structure; intangible assets that don't clearly demonstrate at-risk investment |
| **Platform Flag** | ⚠️ Surface prominent warning: "IT services franchises receive elevated scrutiny for E-2 marginality. Ensure your application demonstrates staffing beyond the investor and active business contracts." |

---

# 7. Automotive Services

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Tangible assets (equipment, bays, vehicles, tools), leased premises, and natural staffing make automotive service a clean fit for E-2; clearly not marginal when capitalized properly |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $200,000 – $500,000+ (equipment and facility are major cost items) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 4–12 (technicians, service advisors, manager) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous; management background more important than mechanical expertise since franchise provides technical training |
| **Example Brands** | Midas, Meineke, Take 5 Oil Change, Tint World, Maaco, Christian Brothers Automotive, Jiffy Lube |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Likely favorable; easy to demonstrate real assets, real jobs, and active operations; weaker only if undercapitalized or no staff plan |
| **Key Risk** | Undercapitalization; lack of operational experience in a technically demanding environment |
| **Platform Flag** | None — Tier 1 category; flag if investment below $150K |

---

# 8. ⚠️ Healthcare Staffing

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | ⚠️ **Moderate / Contested** |
| **Official Basis** | Can qualify as an active staffing company with contracts, recruiting infrastructure, and operational overhead; risk is that initial operations are thin, licensing is complex, and placed workers may not count as company employees for E-2 job creation purposes |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $250,000+ for credible E-2 framing |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 2–8 internal/admin staff (placed workers are separate; internal headcount is what matters for non-marginality argument) |
| **Industry Experience** | Highly advantageous; healthcare staffing is a regulated, compliance-intensive field; lack of relevant experience is a credibility risk at interview |
| **Example Brands** | Interim HealthCare (staffing-side), BrightStar Care (can include staffing component), ATC Healthcare |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Likely wants to see real contracts, licensed operational setup, and internal payroll beyond the investor; weak if presented as speculative with no placements at time of application |
| **Key Risk** | Placed workers ≠ W-2 employees of the franchisor entity; marginality challenge if internal headcount is low; licensing compliance complexity |
| **Platform Flag** | ⚠️ "Healthcare staffing requires particular attention to distinguishing internal W-2 employees from placed workers. Confirm with your immigration attorney how job creation is counted at your specific post." |

---

# 9. Pet Services

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Moderate to Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Storefront, grooming, daycare, and boarding franchises with leased premises and staff qualify well; mobile-only or solo owner-operator models are weaker |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $300,000+ (facility buildout and equipment for daycare/boarding; lower for grooming-only concepts) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 2–8 (groomers, daycare staff, desk staff) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous, not required; franchise training generally comprehensive |
| **Example Brands** | Camp Bow Wow, Dogtopia, Scenthound, Zoom Room, Fetch! Pet Care, Wag N' Wash |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Better when leased site, equipment, and payroll are demonstrated; weaker if business looks too small or heavily owner-operated |
| **Key Risk** | Thin capitalization; solo groomer model without meaningful employees |
| **Platform Flag** | Warn if no physical location or no staff plan |

---

# 10. ⚠️ Real Estate Services (Active, Not Passive)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | ⚠️ **Mixed** |
| **Official Basis** | Active property management (fee-for-service, client contracts, staff) can qualify; passive rental ownership — buying property and collecting rent — does NOT qualify under settled E-2 doctrine; the line between active and passive is scrutinized carefully |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000+ for active service models; passive structures fail regardless of investment size |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 2–6 for property management (minimum; weak cases have zero staff beyond the investor) |
| **Industry Experience** | Highly advantageous; officers scrutinize the active/passive distinction and investor background helps establish credibility |
| **Example Brands** | Property Management Inc., HouseMaster, Pillar To Post, NextHome (service-facing models), Real Property Management |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Officers likely distinguish sharply; active fee-for-service property management with clients and staff should perform better than rental-hold structures, which should be flagged immediately as non-qualifying |
| **Key Risk** | Passive structure; rental-only income; no meaningful internal employees; investor absent from operations |
| **Platform Flag** | ⚠️ "Passive real estate investment (buying and renting property) does not qualify for E-2. Only active real estate service businesses with operational staff and client contracts qualify. Confirm the structure with your immigration attorney before proceeding." |

---

# 11. Retail (Franchise Only)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | **Moderate to Strong** |
| **Official Basis** | Storefront retail with inventory, leased premises, and staff meets the non-marginal and active standards well; kiosk or cart concepts are weaker |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $150,000 – $400,000+ depending on brand, buildout, and initial inventory |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 3–10 (sales staff, shift managers) |
| **Industry Experience** | Advantageous, not required; franchise provides retail training |
| **Example Brands** | The UPS Store, Batteries Plus, Minuteman Press, Fleet Feet, Plato's Closet, Once Upon a Child |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Generally responsive to established franchise retail with lease, opening budget, and staff plan; weak if enterprise appears marginal or is family-only staffed |
| **Key Risk** | Undercapitalization; family-only staffing; kiosk/cart format that looks marginal |
| **Platform Flag** | Warn if kiosk-only or home-based retail concept |

---

# 12. ⚠️ Tax Preparation / Financial Services

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **E-2 Compatibility** | ⚠️ **Uncertain / Contested** |
| **Official Basis** | Can qualify if structured as a real office with licensed staff, recurring client base, and investment beyond personal labor; regularly challenged as professional self-employment — one person doing their own profession — which fails the non-marginal test |
| **Typical Investment Range** | $100,000 – $200,000+ for a stronger E-2 presentation (even if FDD entry cost is lower) |
| **Typical Year 1 Employees** | 1–4 (the low headcount is a standing risk) |
| **Industry Experience** | Often functionally expected; an investor with no financial background in a tax franchise raises credibility questions; licensing requirements vary by state |
| **Example Brands** | H&R Block, Jackson Hewitt, Liberty Tax, Supporting Strategies (bookkeeping), Padgett Business Services |
| **Toronto Pattern** | Likely close scrutiny unless file shows a visible office, multiple staff (not just family), a recurring client base, and clear separation from one-person professional practice |
| **Key Risk** | Solo professional structure; purely personal labor investment; licensing complexity; officer perception as "just working in your profession" |
| **Platform Flag** | ⚠️ "Tax preparation and financial service franchises are frequently challenged under E-2 marginality standards when structured as one-person professional practices. Ensure your application shows staff beyond yourself, a physical office, and a client base that demonstrates non-marginal commercial activity. Confirm viability with your immigration attorney before purchasing." |

## Platform Display Logic

```
Tier 1 categories → Display with green badge "Strong E-2 Fit"
Tier 2 categories → Display with blue badge "Good E-2 Fit — Structure Matters"
Tier 3 categories → Display with yellow/orange badge "⚠️ Elevated Scrutiny — Attorney Review Recommended"

For ALL categories:
  IF projected Year 1 employees == 0 OR 1:
      DISPLAY: "⚠️ E-2 requires a non-marginal enterprise. Single-employee 
      businesses (including owner-only) face elevated denial risk. 
      Ensure your business plan includes a clear hiring plan."

For Real Estate:
  DISPLAY on entry: "Is this an active property management service business or 
  a passive rental investment?"
  IF passive → BLOCK with ineligibility warning
  IF active → PROCEED with ⚠️ structure warning
```
