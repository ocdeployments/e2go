## The Fundamental Issue

The E-2 visa is a **nonimmigrant visa** — it does not lead directly to
permanent resident status (a green card). There is no E-2-to-green-card
conversion. If you want to become a U.S. lawful permanent resident,
you must qualify through a separate immigrant visa pathway while
maintaining valid E-2 status.

> "While E-2 visas are nonimmigrant visas – they do not provide a pathway
> to permanent resident status in the United States – the visa status can
> be extended in perpetuity for as long as the business is in operation."
> — Ford Murray Law (fordmurraylaw.com)

The good news: E-2 status can be maintained **indefinitely** while you
work toward a green card through a qualifying immigrant pathway.

## Pathway 1 — EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program

The most direct investor-to-green-card route for E-2 holders.

### Investment Requirements (as of 2026)

| Investment Location | Minimum Investment | Job Creation Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (non-TEA) | $1,050,000 | 10 full-time U.S. jobs |
| Targeted Employment Area (TEA) — rural or high unemployment | $800,000 | 10 full-time U.S. jobs |

> Source: USCIS — EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program (uscis.gov);
> Ford Murray Law (fordmurraylaw.com)

**Note:** EB-5 investment thresholds are adjusted periodically by USCIS.
Always confirm current amounts at uscis.gov/eb-5 before planning.

### Direct Investment vs. Regional Center

**Direct investment:** You invest directly in your own new commercial
enterprise and create 10 qualifying full-time jobs. Your existing E-2
business may qualify if it meets the investment threshold and job creation
requirements — this is the most natural progression for an E-2 investor
who has grown their business.

**Regional Center investment:** You invest in a USCIS-designated Regional
Center project that pools capital from multiple investors. The minimum
is typically the TEA amount ($800,000). Job creation can be met indirectly
through economic modeling of indirect and induced jobs. You do not need
to manage the Regional Center business.

> "An alternative to direct investment is to invest in an EB-5 Regional
> Center project. Regional Centers are designated by USCIS to promote
> economic growth and can pool investments from multiple investors."
> — SRR Law Group — Pathways from E-2 Visa to Green Card (srrlawgroup.com, 2025)

### EB-5 Process

1. File **Form I-526E** (Regional Center) or **Form I-526** (direct investment)
2. After I-526/I-526E approval: file for immigrant visa abroad (DS-260)
   OR adjust status inside the U.S. (Form I-485) if a visa number is available
3. Receive **conditional green card** (2-year conditional permanent residence)
4. Within 90 days before the 2-year anniversary: file **Form I-829** to
   remove conditions — prove investment was maintained and 10 jobs were created
5. Receive **permanent (unconditional) green card**

### EB-5 Timing for Canadian Nationals

Canadian nationals face **no per-country visa backlog** for EB-5. Canada
is not subject to the annual per-country limits that create multi-year
waits for applicants from China, India, Vietnam, or South Korea.

A Canadian EB-5 applicant can generally expect:
- I-526E/I-526 processing: 12–36 months (varies significantly; premium
  processing is not available for I-526E)
- Consular/AOS processing: 6–12 months after petition approval
- Total to conditional green card: approximately 2–4 years

## Pathway 2 — EB-1C Multinational Manager or Executive

If your E-2 business has a qualifying relationship with a Canadian parent,
subsidiary, or affiliate company, you may qualify for the EB-1C category.

### Requirements

- You must have been employed **abroad** (at the Canadian parent or
  affiliate entity) for at least **1 year within the past 3 years**
  in a **managerial or executive capacity**
- You must be coming to the U.S. to work in a managerial or executive
  capacity for the same employer or a qualifying affiliate
- The **U.S. entity must have been doing business for at least 1 year**
  before the I-140 petition is filed

> Source: USCIS — Employment-Based Immigration: First Preference EB-1C (uscis.gov)

### Why This Works for E-2 Holders

Many Canadian E-2 investors:
1. Operated a Canadian business before their E-2 application
2. Have been operating their U.S. E-2 business for at least 1 year
3. Maintain a qualifying corporate relationship between the Canadian
   and U.S. entities (parent-subsidiary or common ownership affiliate)

This structure may support an EB-1C petition filed after the 1-year
U.S. business operation milestone.

**Key advantages of EB-1C:**
- No labor certification (PERM) required
- No Canadian per-country backlog
- Priority worker category — faster than most employment-based categories
- Typical processing: 12–24 months to green card (with concurrent filing)

**Key limitation:** The managerial/executive definition is strictly
construed by USCIS. You must primarily manage other managers or have
genuine high-level policy-making authority — not just day-to-day
operational management of a small business.

## Pathway 3 — EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW)

An EB-2 NIW allows highly qualified individuals to self-petition for a
green card without employer sponsorship and without PERM labor certification,
if their work serves the **national interest of the United States.**

### The Three-Prong Test (Matter of Dhanasar, 2016)

1. The proposed endeavor has **substantial merit and national importance**
2. The applicant is **well-positioned to advance** the proposed endeavor
3. On balance, it would be **beneficial to the United States** to waive
   the job offer and labor certification requirements

> Source: USCIS — EB-2 National Interest Waiver; Matter of Dhanasar, 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016)

### Who This Applies To Among E-2 Holders

NIW is available to E-2 investors who are independently accomplished
professionals — scientists, researchers, physicians, engineers, entrepreneurs
in technology, clean energy, or healthcare with documented exceptional work.

**For most E-2 franchise or service business investors:** NIW is unlikely
to apply. The standard focuses on the individual's specific professional
contributions to national interests — not on operating a commercial business.

**For E-2 investors with a technology or innovation focus** (health tech,
clean energy, advanced manufacturing, AI): NIW may be worth evaluating
with an immigration attorney.

## Pathway 4 — PERM / EB-2 or EB-3 Employer Sponsorship

Standard employment-based green card requiring:
- A qualifying U.S. employer to sponsor you
- PERM labor certification (the employer conducts a recruitment process
  to demonstrate no qualified U.S. workers are available)
- Form I-140 immigrant petition

**Sponsoring yourself** through your own E-2 business is legally possible
but complex — USCIS scrutinizes self-petitions carefully. You must
demonstrate that the ownership and management roles are genuinely separate
and that the recruitment process was bona fide.

**Canadian advantage:** No per-country backlog for EB-2 or EB-3.

## Pathway 5 — Family-Based Sponsorship

If a close U.S. citizen relative can sponsor you:

| Relationship | Category | Backlog (Canada) |
|---|---|---|
| U.S. citizen spouse | Immediate Relative (IR-1) | None — priority processing |
| U.S. citizen parent (petitioning for adult child) | Immediate Relative | None |
| U.S. citizen adult child (petitioning for parent) | Immediate Relative | None |
| Lawful Permanent Resident spouse | F-2A | Modest wait |
| U.S. citizen sibling | F-4 preference | Multi-year wait |

> Source: Travel.State.Gov — Visa Bulletin (travel.state.gov)

## Pathway 6 — L-1A to EB-1C (Structured Build)

Some E-2 investors who have both a Canadian and a U.S. entity restructure
their path:
1. Transfer to the U.S. on **L-1A status** (intracompany transferee —
   manager/executive) using the qualifying corporate relationship
2. After 1 year of U.S. operations, file EB-1C green card petition
3. L-1A can be maintained while the EB-1C is pending

> "E-2 visa holders who own or work for multinational companies can also
> consider the L-1 visa as a pathway to a green card... After obtaining
> an L-1 visa, the individual can transition to an EB-1C green card."
> — SRR Law Group (srrlawgroup.com)

## Critical Warning — Immigrant Intent and E-2 Renewals

Once you file an immigrant visa petition (I-526, I-526E, or I-140), this
is evidence of immigrant intent — the same standard consular officers
evaluate when reviewing E-2 visa applications and renewals.

> "An approved immigrant visa petition in itself is not a bar to issuance
> of a nonimmigrant visa, but it is a strong indicator of an intention to
> remain permanently in the U.S."
> — 9 FAM 302.1-2(B)(4)

A pending immigrant petition does **not automatically disqualify** you
from E-2 renewal, but it creates a burden to demonstrate present
nonimmigrant intent. Officers will scrutinize your application more closely.

**The sequencing and timing of immigrant petitions while on E-2 status
requires coordination with a qualified immigration attorney.** Do not
file any immigrant petition without first discussing the impact on your
E-2 status with counsel.
