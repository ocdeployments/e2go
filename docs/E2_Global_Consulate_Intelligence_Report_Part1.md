### Notes on Treaty Data
- Source: [U.S. Department of State — Treaty Countries](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/fees/treaty.html)
- E-2 treaty dates reflect when the treaty entered into force with the United States.
- India is **NOT** an E-2 treaty country. See Appendix A for non-treaty national guidance.
- Pakistan and Sri Lanka are **confirmed E-2 treaty countries** per official State Department records.
- As of September 6, 2025, third-country national processing has been eliminated. Applicants must apply at a U.S. consulate in their **country of nationality or legal residence**. 🗓️ Annual Review Required

## Key Policy Update — Third-Country National Processing

> 🗓️ Annual Review Required

As of **September 6, 2025**, the U.S. Department of State eliminated the practice of allowing E-2 applicants to apply at a U.S. consulate in a third country. Previously, applicants could schedule at more convenient or faster consular posts outside their home country. This is no longer permitted.

**Platform implication:** Remove any interface option that allows users to select a processing post other than their country of nationality or lawful residence. Surface a clear warning if a user's selected post does not match their nationality.
