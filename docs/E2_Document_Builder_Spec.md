# SECTION 2 — Cover Letter Specifications

## Universal Cover Letter Structure
```
[Applicant Full Name]
[Applicant Address]
[Date]

Nonimmigrant Visa Unit
[U.S. Embassy / Consulate Name]
[Embassy Address]

Re: E-2 Treaty Investor Visa Application
Nationality: [Treaty Country]
Treaty Basis: [Treaty Name and Date]

Dear Visa Officer,

PARAGRAPH 1 — Treaty Basis
I am a national of [Country], which maintains an E-2 Treaty of Commerce and Navigation
with the United States, in force since [Treaty Date]. I am applying for an E-2 Treaty
Investor visa pursuant to INA §101(a)(15)(E)(ii).

PARAGRAPH 2 — Investment Summary
I have invested / am in the process of investing [Amount USD] in [Business Name], a
[Business Type] located at [U.S. Business Address]. The investment is substantial,
at-risk, and not marginal.

PARAGRAPH 3 — Business Description
[Business Name] is engaged in [core activity]. The business will [description of
operations, products/services, target market, revenue model].

PARAGRAPH 4 — Investor Role
I will direct and develop the enterprise as [Title]. My day-to-day responsibilities
include [list of active management duties]. I am not a passive investor.

PARAGRAPH 5 — Job Creation
The enterprise will create employment for U.S. workers. [Current/projected employee count
and roles]. This demonstrates the non-marginal nature of the investment.

PARAGRAPH 6 — Documents Enclosed
Enclosed please find the following supporting documents: [numbered list from master checklist].

Respectfully submitted,
[Signature]
[Full Name]
[Date]
```

## Post-Specific Cover Letter Variations

**Frankfurt:**
- Add explicit page count: "This application package contains [X] pages, within the 30-page limit."
- Compress all narrative — Frankfurt version is 1 page maximum

**Toronto:**
- Add: "My spouse [Name] and dependent children [Names] will attend the interview."
- Reference CUSMA/USMCA treaty explicitly: "pursuant to the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA/CUSMA)"

**Seoul:**
- Reference completed SEO supplement: "I have completed the State Department Seoul post supplement enclosed herewith."

**Pakistan (Islamabad):**
- Note nonimmigrant intent clearly: "I am applying for a nonimmigrant E-2 visa."
- No reference to immigrant visa pause needed in cover letter itself

# SECTION 3 — Business Plan Specifications

## Standard Business Plan Structure (All Posts Except Frankfurt)

```
SECTION 1: Executive Summary (1 page)
  - Business name, type, location
  - Investment amount
  - Treaty nationality
  - Role of investor
  - Job creation summary

SECTION 2: Business Description (2-3 pages)
  - Nature of business
  - Products / services
  - Target market
  - Competitive advantage
  - Location and facilities

SECTION 3: Investor Background (1-2 pages)
  - Professional history
  - Industry experience relevant to this business
  - Why qualified to manage this enterprise

SECTION 4: Investment Details (2-3 pages)
  - Total investment amount
  - What has been invested / committed to date
  - Breakdown of use of funds
  - Evidence investment is at risk
  - Source of funds summary (with reference to attached documentation)

SECTION 5: Organizational Structure (1 page)
  - Business entity type
  - Ownership structure
  - Investor's ownership percentage
  - Management structure

SECTION 6: Job Creation Plan (1-2 pages)
  - Current employees (if any)
  - Projected hiring plan (Year 1, Year 2, Year 3)
  - Job titles and descriptions
  - U.S. citizen / permanent resident hiring emphasis
  - Compensation structure

SECTION 7: Financial Projections (2-3 pages)
  - Revenue projections (3-5 years)
  - Expense projections
  - EBITDA / profit projections
  - Key assumptions
  - Break-even analysis

SECTION 8: Market Analysis (1-2 pages)
  - Industry overview
  - Target customer profile
  - Market size
  - Competition analysis

TOTAL TARGET: 12-18 pages standard
```

## Frankfurt Business Plan Structure (30-Page Total Package Hard Limit)

```
Frankfurt business plan = EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ONLY

MAXIMUM: 4-5 pages total for business plan section

INCLUDE:
  - Business name, type, location (1 paragraph)
  - Investment amount and breakdown (bullet list)
  - Investor role (1 paragraph)
  - Job creation summary (bullet list — Year 1 and Year 3 projections)
  - Revenue projections (single table — 3 years)
  - Key market context (2-3 sentences only)

OMIT (due to page constraint):
  - Extended narrative market analysis
  - Detailed competitive analysis
  - Extended investor biography (reduce to 1 paragraph)

PLATFORM BEHAVIOR: When post == Frankfurt, generate condensed template automatically
```

## Business Plan — Investment Type Variations

**Franchise Business Plan:**
  - Add: Franchise brand overview (1 paragraph)
  - Add: FDD summary reference (reference attached FDD, do not reproduce)
  - Add: Franchise territory and exclusivity
  - Add: Franchisor support and training
  - Add: Franchisee investment breakdown per FDD Item 7

**Acquisition of Existing Business:**
  - Add: Business valuation summary
  - Add: Purchase price allocation
  - Add: Historical financials of acquired business (3 years)
  - Add: Post-acquisition operating plan
  - Add: Why current owner is selling

**Startup / New Business:**
  - Emphasize: Why this investor has relevant experience
  - Emphasize: Market research basis for projections
  - Note: Higher scrutiny on projections — must be defensible

# SECTION 4 — Source of Funds Documentation Guide

## Universal Source of Funds Requirements
```
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR ALL POSTS:
1. Bank statements — 12 months minimum; 24 months preferred
2. Tax returns — 3 years
3. Evidence of lawful origin of funds:
   - Employment income: pay stubs, employment contracts, W-2 or local equivalent
   - Business income: corporate tax returns, profit distributions, dividend records
   - Real estate sale: closing documents, title transfer, net proceeds statement
   - Investment liquidation: brokerage statements showing sale proceeds
   - Inheritance/gift: probate documents, gift letter, estate documents
4. Wire transfer records showing funds moved to U.S. investment
5. Escrow confirmation (if applicable)
```

## Country-Specific Additions

**Pakistan:**
  - FBR (Federal Board of Revenue) tax return copies (Form Income Tax Return)
  - Pakistani bank statements with English headers or certified translation
  - Currency conversion: show official PKR/USD exchange rate at date of transfer

**Germany:**
  - Steuerbescheid (tax assessment notice) — 3 years
  - Kontoauszüge (bank statements) — 12 months minimum
  - Certified English translation required for all

**South Korea:**
  - Korean National Tax Service (NTS) tax records
  - Korean bank statements with certified English translation
  - Apostille may be required on Korean government documents 🔄 Verify

**Japan:**
  - Kakuteishinkoku (tax return) — 3 years
  - Bank passbook copies (tsuuchō) or online statement printouts
  - Certified English translation required for all

**France:**
  - Avis d'imposition (tax assessment) — 3 years
  - Relevés bancaires (bank statements) — 12 months
  - Certified English translation required

**Italy:**
  - Dichiarazione dei redditi (tax return) — 3 years
  - Estratto conto bancario (bank statements) — 12 months
  - Certified English translation + apostille on government documents

**Argentina:**
  - AFIP tax return — 3 years
  - Argentine bank statements — 12 months
  - Currency documentation: ARS to USD conversion with official exchange rate
  - Note: Argentine currency volatility — document value at multiple dates

**Egypt:**
  - Egyptian Tax Authority records — 3 years
  - Egyptian bank statements — 12 months
  - Certified English translation; notarization may be required 🔄 Verify
  - EGP to USD conversion documented

**Panama:**
  - Bank statements — 24 months minimum (higher standard given offshore context)
  - Tax returns — 3 years
  - ⚠️ If funds pass through offshore entities: complete paper trail required showing clean origin
  - Attorney letter addressing source of funds strongly recommended

**Colombia:**
  - DIAN tax return — 3 years
  - Colombian bank statements — 12 months
  - Certified English translation
  - COP to USD conversion documented

# SECTION 5 — Interview Preparation Guide (Per Post)

## Universal Interview Prep — Core Questions

Every applicant must be able to answer clearly and concisely:

```
1. "Describe your business."
   → Practice: 60-second clear description; business type, location, what it does, who it serves

2. "How much have you invested?"
   → Know exact dollar amount; be ready to explain breakdown

3. "Where did the money come from?"
   → Practice complete source-of-funds narrative; match documents in package

4. "What is your day-to-day role in the business?"
   → Describe active management duties specifically — NOT passive investor

5. "How many U.S. workers will you hire?"
   → Know Year 1 and Year 3 hiring plan from business plan

6. "What experience do you have in this industry?"
   → Connect professional background directly to business type

7. "Where is the business located?"
   → Know exact address; have lease or purchase agreement

8. "What happens to the business if your visa is denied?"
   → Honest answer about impact; business is already invested / at risk

9. "Have you visited the United States before?"
   → Be prepared to discuss travel history

10. "What is your intended length of stay?"
    → Understand E-2 duration; "I will renew as needed while building the business"
```

## Post-Specific Interview Notes

**Toronto:**
- Officers are methodical and detailed — expect questions on every line of financial projections
- Family members may be briefly greeted/acknowledged — they do not need to speak at length
- Organized binder is noticed — disorganized submissions reflect on applicant

**Frankfurt:**
- Interview is brief — efficient and to the point
- Officer has limited pages to review — be prepared for questions based on condensed package
- German directness — answer questions directly without over-explaining

**Istanbul:**
- Very fast appointment availability — but officers experienced with CBI pathway applicants
- If recently naturalized Turkish: be prepared to clearly explain naturalization timeline and history

**Pakistan (Islamabad):**
- Source of funds will be probed carefully
- Be prepared to walk through every major transfer in the financial paper trail
- Admin processing (221g) is possible — don't panic; plan for longer timeline

**Colombo (Sri Lanka):**
- Bring DS-160 confirmation page — verify barcode matches appointment BEFORE interview day
- English-language interview — no interpreter needed for most Sri Lankan applicants

**Seoul:**
- Bring printed copy of completed SEO supplement
- Officers familiar with franchise and restaurant investments — common at this post
- Translation quality matters — use certified professional translators
