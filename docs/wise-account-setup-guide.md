## What Is Wise Business?

Wise (formerly TransferWise) is an international money services platform
that offers a **multi-currency business account** with real account details
in multiple countries — including a real U.S. routing number and account number.

Wise Business is not a traditional FDIC-chartered bank. It is a licensed
money services business that holds client funds in safeguarded accounts
at partner banks.

For Canadian E-2 applicants, Wise Business fills a critical gap:
**you can open a functional U.S. business account with real U.S. bank
details (routing + account number) entirely from Canada, before you have
a U.S. address or U.S. presence.**

## What You Get With Wise Business

- **Real U.S. bank account details** — U.S. ABA routing number and account

  convert at the real mid-market rate

- **International transfers** — send money to 80+ countries at low cost
- **No monthly fee** (one-time setup fee only)

## Who Can Open a Wise Business Account

Wise Business is available to:
- Canadian residents operating a business
- Non-U.S. residents with a registered U.S. LLC
- Sole proprietors, partnerships, corporations, and LLCs

**You do NOT need:**
- A U.S. address
- A U.S. Social Security Number
- An existing U.S. bank account
- U.S. citizenship or permanent residency

## Requirements — Wise Business Account for a U.S. LLC

To open Wise Business for a U.S. LLC from Canada, you need:

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| **1. Government-issued photo ID** | Canadian passport (preferred) or Canadian driver's licence |
| **2. Articles of Organization** | Filed copy from the Secretary of State — confirms LLC exists |
| **3. EIN (Employer Identification Number)** | Required — obtain from IRS before applying |
| **4. Operating Agreement** | May be requested during verification |
| **5. U.S. business address** | Physical address — registered agent address acceptable |
| **6. Your personal address** | Your Canadian home address — not a P.O. Box |
| **7. Business description** | Brief description of what the business does |

> Source: Wise YouTube tutorial; Wise — Business Account Requirements (wise.com/us/blog, 2023)

**Critical prerequisite:** You **must have your EIN** before applying to Wise.
Without an EIN, Wise cannot verify your U.S. LLC and will not open the account.
Obtain your EIN by phone from Canada (see `kb/us-llc-formation-from-canada.md`)
before starting the Wise application.

## Step-by-Step: Opening a Wise Business Account

### Step 1 — Go to wise.com/business

Navigate to wise.com/business or wise.com/ca/business (Canadian version).
Click "Open a Business Account."

### Step 2 — Create Your Wise Profile

- Enter your personal email address
- Create a password
- Verify your email address

### Step 3 — Select Account Type

Select:
- Business type: **LLC**
- Country of registration: **United States**
- If asked about your personal country: **Canada**

### Step 4 — Enter Business Details

You will be asked for:
- Legal business name (exactly as on Articles of Organization)
- State of registration
- EIN (enter as XX-XXXXXXX format)
- Business address (U.S. — registered agent or business address)
- Business phone number
- Business category / industry type
- Brief business description (be specific — "residential cleaning franchise
  in [city, state]" is better than "cleaning services")

### Step 5 — Enter Owner Information

For each owner with 25%+ ownership:
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Nationality (Canadian)
- Canadian home address
- Percentage of ownership

### Step 6 — Identity Verification

Wise requires identity verification through their automated system:
- Upload a scan of your Canadian passport (front page)
- Complete a video selfie or photo verification (confirms passport matches face)
- This is done through the Wise app on your phone — you will receive a link

> ⚠️ Wise's previous process allowed direct document upload on desktop.
> As of 2023+, verification is increasingly done through the mobile app
> for security. Have your phone ready when you apply on desktop.

### Step 7 — Pay the Setup Fee

One-time account setup fee:
- **CAD $55** (Canadian Wise Business)
- Paid by credit or debit card

### Step 8 — Account Review

Wise reviews your application. Typical approval time:
- **Standard:** 1–3 business days
- Can be longer if additional documents are requested

### Step 9 — Activate Your USD Account Details

Once approved:
- Navigate to the "Add money" or "Account details" section
- Request your **USD account details** (routing number + account number)
- These are your real U.S. banking credentials

## What Wise Is Good For — E-2 Applicant Use Cases

### ✅ Strong Use Cases

**Receiving your initial investment transfer from Canada:**
Use Wise to receive your initial E-2 investment capital transferred from
your Canadian personal or business accounts. This creates a clean,
documented transfer record — critical for E-2 source of funds documentation.

**Paying U.S. vendors and suppliers (pre-arrival):**
Pay your registered agent annual fee, state filing fees, online vendors,
and U.S. suppliers from Canada before you have a traditional U.S. bank account.

**Paying international invoices:**
Pay Canadian suppliers or foreign vendors in their local currency at
competitive exchange rates.

**Cross-border payroll (if applicable):**
Pay Canadian contractors from your U.S. LLC using Wise's multi-currency
transfer capability.

**Receiving U.S. ACH payments:**
Any U.S. payer that can send an ACH transfer can pay your Wise USD account
directly using your routing and account number.

### ⚠️ Limitations to Plan Around

**Not accepted by all traditional U.S. businesses:**
Some U.S. vendors, insurance companies, payroll processors, and government
agencies require a traditional FDIC-chartered bank account. A Wise account
number from a non-bank may be rejected by:
- Certain payroll processors (ADP, Gusto require traditional bank in some configurations)
- Some landlords for pre-authorized rent payments
- Some state government agencies for tax remittances

**No cash deposits:**
Wise has no physical branches. You cannot deposit U.S. cash (from your
business) into a Wise account. If your E-2 business handles any cash,
you will need a traditional bank account for cash deposits.

**Not ideal as your permanent business account:**
Plan to open a Chase, Bank of America, or local U.S. business bank account
after arriving in the U.S. Use Wise as a bridge account for pre-arrival
banking and ongoing international transfers.

**FDIC insurance:** Wise is not a bank and your funds are not FDIC insured
through Wise directly. Wise holds funds with FDIC-insured partner banks under
a safeguarding model. Understand this distinction before depositing large sums.

## Using Wise for E-2 Source of Funds Documentation

One of the strongest features of Wise for E-2 applicants is the clean
transfer documentation it generates.

For E-2, you must trace investment funds from your personal savings to the
U.S. business account. A typical paper trail might look like:

```
Canadian personal savings account (RBC Royal Bank)
    ↓  [Bank statement showing transfer]
Wise Business Account (USD)
    ↓  [Wise transaction record]
U.S. business LLC account (Chase / Bank of America)
    ↓  [Business bank statement]
Business expenses (rent, equipment, franchise fee, inventory)
    ↓  [Invoices, receipts, contracts]
```

Wise generates downloadable transaction statements that show:
- Transfer date
- Amount in source and destination currency
- Exchange rate applied
- Sender and recipient details

These statements are accepted as documentary evidence in E-2 applications
to demonstrate the movement of funds from the investor's personal accounts
to the U.S. business.

## Troubleshooting Common Wise Application Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Verification stuck / app won't load | Use the Wise mobile app, not desktop browser, for selfie verification |
| EIN not accepted | Ensure EIN is entered as XX-XXXXXXX (with hyphen); use the EIN exactly as issued by IRS |
| Address rejected | Ensure U.S. address is a physical location (not P.O. Box); registered agent address is acceptable |
| Additional documents requested | Respond quickly with clear scans; delays extend review time |
| Account opened but USD details not available | Request USD account details explicitly in the account — they are not automatic in all regions |
