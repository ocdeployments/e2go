## 10.1 Go-To-Market Launch Sequence

### Launch Philosophy

E2Pathway is not a mass-market product. The total addressable market
is Canadians actively planning an E-2 visa application — a defined,
reachable, high-intent audience. The launch strategy therefore
prioritizes depth over breadth: find the exact people who are already
searching, already in Facebook groups, already reading immigration blogs,
and be the most helpful thing they encounter.

The launch sequence runs in 5 phases over 90 days.

---

### Phase 0 — Pre-Launch (Weeks -8 to -1)

Goal: Build a warm audience before the product is live.
Target: 500 email subscribers before launch day.

Actions:

1. LANDING PAGE (Week -8)
   Launch a single-page site with:
   - Headline: "The Complete E-2 Visa Preparation System for Canadians"
   - Sub-headline: "Guided document preparation, interview simulation,
     and a compliance calendar — built specifically for Canadians
     applying at the Toronto consulate."
   - Email capture: "Join the waitlist — get early access and a
     launch discount."
   - Social proof placeholder: "Currently in development. First 100
     subscribers get 30% off at launch."
   - FAQ section addressing the top 5 questions from Facebook group research

2. CONTENT SEEDING (Weeks -8 to -1)
   Publish 3 high-value blog posts before launch:
   - "The 7 Most Common Reasons Canadians Get Denied an E-2 Visa
     (and How to Avoid Each One)"
   - "How Much Do You Actually Need to Invest for an E-2 Visa?
     The Proportionality Test Explained"
   - "E-2 Visa vs. EB-5: Which Path Makes Sense for Canadians
     Moving to the U.S.?"
   Each post ends with the email capture form.

3. FACEBOOK GROUP PRESENCE (Weeks -6 to -1)
   Target groups: "Canadians Moving to Florida", "E-2 Visa Community",
   "Canadian Expats in the USA"
   Approach: Answer questions genuinely and helpfully — never pitch.
   Build a reputation as a knowledgeable resource BEFORE mentioning
   the product. After 3–4 weeks of helpful participation, a soft
   mention of the waitlist is appropriate.

4. WAITLIST NURTURE (Weeks -4 to -1)
   Send 2 emails to waitlist subscribers:
   Email 1 (Week -4): "Here's what we're building and why"
   Email 2 (Week -1): "You're on the list — launch is in 7 days.
   Here's your early-access discount code."

---

### Phase 1 — Launch Week (Days 1–7)

Goal: First 20 paying subscribers.

Day 1 — Launch Email (to full waitlist):
Subject: "E2Pathway is live — your 30% discount expires in 48 hours"
- Announce launch
- Show pricing with discount applied
- 3-sentence product description
- One testimonial from beta user (if available) or founder's own story
- CTA: [Start My E-2 Application →]

Day 1 — Organic Social Posts:
- LinkedIn: Founder post explaining why E2Pathway was built
  (personal story, specific pain point, credibility)
- Facebook groups: Announce launch with a helpful framing
  ("I built this because I kept seeing the same mistakes...")

Day 3 — Follow-Up Email (to non-openers):
Subject: "Did you see this? E2Pathway is live."
Same content, different subject line. Sent to subscribers who
did not open Day 1 email.

Day 5 — Urgency Email:
Subject: "48 hours left on your launch discount"
- Brief reminder
- Add one new piece of social proof if available
- Hard deadline countdown

Day 7 — Final Email:
Subject: "Last chance — discount expires tonight"
- 1-paragraph reminder
- CTA to subscribe before midnight

---

### Phase 2 — Early Adopter Feedback (Days 8–30)

Goal: 50 total subscribers. Gather first user feedback.
Identify any UX issues, missing content, or friction points.

Actions:

1. ONBOARDING EMAIL SEQUENCE ACTIVATED (see Section 10.2)
   All new subscribers enter the 10-email nurture sequence.

2. FOUNDER OUTREACH TO FIRST 20 SUBSCRIBERS
   Personal email to each of the first 20 paying subscribers:
   "Hi [name], I'm [founder name], the person who built E2Pathway.
   You're one of our first 20 subscribers. I'd love to jump on a
   15-minute Zoom call to hear about your situation and make sure
   the app is serving you well. Would that work?"
   Goal: 60% call completion rate. Each call surfaces product gaps.

3. BETA TESTIMONIAL COLLECTION
   After 2 weeks of use, ask first 20 subscribers for a testimonial.
   Even a 1-sentence quote is valuable at this stage.

4. PRODUCT ITERATION
   Based on first 20 user calls, identify top 3 issues and fix them
   before Phase 3 begins.

---

### Phase 3 — Content Velocity (Days 31–60)

Goal: 150 total subscribers. Build organic search traffic.

Actions:

1. CONTENT PUBLISHING — 2 posts per week
   Target keywords identified from SEO strategy (Section 10.4):
   - "E-2 visa Canada Toronto"
   - "E-2 visa business plan requirements"
   - "E-2 visa source of funds Canada"
   - "E-2 visa denial reasons"
   - "E-2 visa franchise Canada"
   - "E-2 visa renewal Toronto"
   Each post is a definitive guide — 1,500–3,000 words,
   not a thin summary.

2. ATTORNEY & FRANCHISE PARTNER OUTREACH (see Section 10.5)
   Begin outreach to 10 immigration attorneys and 5 franchise
   broker networks. Goal: 2 referral partnerships active by Day 60.

3. YOUTUBE / LOOM VIDEO CONTENT
   Record 4 explainer videos:
   - "E-2 Visa Explained for Canadians — Everything You Need to Know"
   - "How Much Investment is Enough for an E-2 Visa?"
   - "The Toronto Consulate Interview — What to Expect"
   - "E-2 Visa Business Plan — What Officers Actually Look For"
   Post to YouTube. Embed in corresponding blog posts.

4. REDDIT PRESENCE
   r/ImmigrationCanada, r/PersonalFinanceCanada, r/canadatousa
   Same approach as Facebook: answer questions helpfully first.
   Mention E2Pathway only when directly relevant.

---

### Phase 4 — Partnership Activation (Days 61–90)

Goal: 300 total subscribers. First referral partnerships generating leads.

Actions:

1. FIRST ATTORNEY PARTNERSHIP LIVE
   Attorney partner promotes E2Pathway to their prospective clients
   as a preparation tool ("get organized before your consult").
   In return, E2Pathway routes attorney referrals to this partner.
   Revenue share or flat referral fee negotiated per partnership.

2. FIRST FRANCHISE BROKER PARTNERSHIP LIVE
   Franchise broker promotes E2Pathway to Canadian E-2 prospects.
   In return, E2Pathway routes franchise referrals to this broker.

3. CANADIAN EXPAT COMMUNITY EVENTS
   Identify online and in-person events where Canadian expat
   communities gather:
   - Canadian-American Professional Association events
   - SCORE small business workshops
   - Franchise expos (IFE, Franchise Expo South)
   Attend or sponsor as appropriate.

4. AFFILIATE PROGRAM SOFT LAUNCH
   Launch a simple affiliate program for Canadian immigration
   bloggers, YouTube channels, and Facebook group admins:
   - 20% commission on first-year subscription
   - Unique tracking link per affiliate
   - Monthly payout via Stripe

---

### Phase 5 — Optimization & Scale (Day 90+)

Goal: 500+ total subscribers. Positive unit economics confirmed.

Actions:

1. PAID SEARCH (Google Ads)
   Launch only once organic content is established and conversion
   rate is proven (target: >3% landing page → subscriber).
   Initial budget: $500–$1,000 CAD/month.
   Target keywords: "e2 visa canada", "e2 visa toronto consulate",
   "e2 visa business plan canada"

2. RETARGETING
   Retarget blog readers who did not subscribe using Google Display
   or Facebook retargeting.
   Budget: $200–$300 CAD/month.

3. TESTIMONIAL-LED SOCIAL ADS
   Once 10+ verified testimonials are collected, build social ads
   around real user stories.
   "David from Ontario got his E-2 in 74 days. Here's how he prepared."

4. SEO COMPOUNDING
   By Month 6, organic search should be generating 40–60% of
   new subscriber traffic from content published in Phases 3 and 4.

## 10.2 Email Nurture Sequence — 10 Emails

### Purpose
Every new subscriber (paying or waitlist) enters a 10-email nurture
sequence. For paying subscribers, this sequence runs alongside their
active use of the app — reinforcing learning and keeping users engaged.
For waitlist and free-trial users, it serves as a conversion tool.

The tone is educational, warm, and specific — not generic marketing.
Every email teaches something real about the E-2 visa process.
The product is mentioned naturally, never forced.

---

### Email 1 — Day 0 (Immediate After Signup)
Subject: "Welcome — here's what matters most in the next 90 days"

Opening:
"You're now part of a small, determined group of Canadians who are
serious about building something in the United States. That matters.
Most people talk about it. You're actually doing it.

Here's what I want you to know right now, before we talk about anything else:"

Core content:
- The single biggest mistake Canadians make (selling the Canadian home too early)
- Why the Toronto consulate is more straightforward than most applicants expect
- What the next 90 days should look like at a high level

Closing CTA (for non-subscribers): [Start Your Application →]
Closing CTA (for subscribers): [Open the App and Start Module 1 →]

---

### Email 2 — Day 2
Subject: "The substantiality question — what 'enough' actually means"

Core content:
- Explain the proportionality test in plain English
- The three investment brackets and what each requires
- The Toronto consulate's observed $75K–$100K practical floor
- Common mistake: confusing total net worth with investment amount

Teaching example:
"If you're buying a franchise that costs $200,000 total, and you're
putting in $155,000 — that's 77.5% of the total cost. That's well
into the 'strong' range for a medium-sized business investment."

Subtle CTA: "Our investment substantiality calculator in Module 1
walks you through this automatically — including the CAD/USD
conversion at today's rate."

---

### Email 3 — Day 5
Subject: "The hardest part of any E-2 application (and it's not the business plan)"

Core content:
- Source of funds is the #1 cause of 221(g) supplemental requests
- What "tracing" actually means — why gaps matter
- The 3 cleanest fund sources and why property sales need extra documentation
- The gift trap: why gifted funds need to show the donor's source too

Teaching example:
"I've seen applications with excellent business plans denied at the
supplement stage because the applicant couldn't explain why $80,000
moved from one account to another without documentation. The business
plan wasn't the problem. The paper trail was."

Subtle CTA: "Template 1 in the app builds your funds chronology
automatically — including alerts for gaps that officers commonly flag."

---

### Email 4 — Day 8
Subject: "What the officer is really asking when they say 'Tell me about your business'"

Core content:
- The actual subtext behind the 6 most common interview questions
- What "develop and direct" really means in practice
- Why passive-sounding language kills applications
- The management role framing — how to describe your role convincingly

Example:
"'Tell me about your business' is not a small talk question. It's
an assessment of whether you actually understand the enterprise you're
investing in. The officer wants to hear: what it does, who the customers
are, how it makes money, and why you specifically are the right person
to run it. In 90 seconds or less."

Subtle CTA: "The interview simulator in the app runs you through this
question with your specific business details — and gives you immediate
feedback on your answer."

---

### Email 5 — Day 12
Subject: "The 214(b) trap — and how Canadians accidentally fall into it"

Core content:
- What 214(b) is and why it is the most dangerous denial category
- The 5 actions that create immigrant intent signals
- The Canadian home sale timing problem in detail
- How to maintain Canadian ties while genuinely planning to live in the U.S.

This email is the most emotionally resonant — most Canadians
have already done at least one thing on the list.

Opening:
"I want to talk about something most E-2 guides skip over because
it's uncomfortable. The 214(b) denial. It's the one that says,
in plain English: 'We don't believe you're planning to come back.'"

Subtle CTA: "The immigrant intent risk assessment in Module 1
flags these situations early — before they become problems."

---

### Email 6 — Day 16
Subject: "Why your business plan is making the officer's eyes glaze over"

Core content:
- The difference between a business plan written for investors
  and one written for a consular officer
- The 5 things officers actually look for
- Why generic market statistics fail and local data wins
- The non-marginality math — show it explicitly

Example of weak vs. strong:
Weak: "The home care industry is expected to grow at 7% annually."
Strong: "There are 47,000 adults aged 65+ within 10 miles of our
Tampa location. The county's senior population grew 14% from
2020 to 2024. We are targeting 0.6% market penetration in Year 1."

Subtle CTA: "Template 2 in the app generates your business plan
using your specific inputs — city-level market data, your actual
projections, your real hiring plan."

---

### Email 7 — Day 21
Subject: "The 11-tab binder — what goes in each one (and what kills applications)"

Core content:
- Walk through each of the 11 tabs at the Toronto consulate
- The 3 tabs that most applications get wrong (H, I, and D)
- The 50-page-per-tab limit and how to manage it
- What "Tab K — Business Plan" actually needs to contain

This is the most practical email in the sequence — high open rate
expected because it directly answers a specific, frequent question.

Subtle CTA: "The app generates pre-formatted content for Tabs D, H, I,
and K automatically. The binder checklist walks you through the rest."

---

### Email 8 — Day 28
Subject: "Approved in 74 days — [first name]'s E-2 story"

Core content:
- A real user story (once testimonials are available)
  or a realistic composite until then
- Walk through their specific situation: business type, investment,
  interview date, outcome
- What they found hardest and how they overcame it
- A direct quote

If no real testimonial yet, use founder story or a "this is what
success looks like" hypothetical framed clearly as illustrative.

Subtle CTA: "[Start your application →] — we're ready when you are."

---

### Email 9 — Day 35
Subject: "The renewal trap — what happens if your business doesn't hit its projections"

Core content:
- Why renewal is harder than the original application for some people
- The actual vs. projected comparison at Year 5
- What officers look for at renewal that they don't at original application
- The marginality risk at renewal and the 3x household income rule
- Why building a good record from Year 1 matters for Year 5

This email creates long-term product value by educating users that
the journey doesn't end at approval — and that E2Pathway
supports them through renewal.

Subtle CTA: "The compliance calendar in the app starts tracking your
renewal readiness at Year 3 — so you're never caught off guard."

---

### Email 10 — Day 42
Subject: "You're more ready than you think — here's your next step"

Core content:
- Summarize what the user now knows (9 emails of real education)
- Address the most common hesitation: "I'm not ready yet"
  (reframe: you will never feel completely ready; preparation
  is what creates readiness)
- One paragraph on what the application process actually looks like
  end to end (timeline, steps)
- A direct, warm, non-pushy close

Closing:
"The Canadians who get their E-2 visa aren't the ones who waited until
everything felt perfect. They're the ones who started organizing, kept
going even when it felt overwhelming, and showed up to their interview
having done the work.

You've read 9 emails. You know more than most applicants who walk
into that consulate. The next step is to start."

CTA: [Open E2Pathway and Start Module 1 →]
(For subscribers already active): [Continue where you left off →]

---

### Sequence Technical Notes

Platform: Email service provider with automation (Resend + React Email
for transactional; ConvertKit or Loops for nurture sequence)

Segmentation:
- Paying subscribers receive emails 1–10 with "subscriber" CTAs
- Non-paying (waitlist/free) receive same content with "subscribe" CTAs
- Users who subscribe during the sequence are moved to subscriber variant
  automatically

Timing:
- Emails 1–5: every 2–3 days (high-momentum early engagement)
- Emails 6–10: every 5–7 days (sustaining without overwhelming)

Open rate targets:
- Email 1: 55%+ (new subscriber peak)
- Emails 2–5: 35–45%
- Emails 6–10: 25–35%

After email 10, subscribers move to:
- Monthly "E-2 Update" newsletter (CASL-consented only)
- Compliance calendar reminder emails (active subscribers only)
- Renewal reminder sequence (Year 4 trigger, active subscribers)
