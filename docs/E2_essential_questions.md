# Essential E‑2 Visa Questions (Official‑Source Aligned)

*Scope: Core questions every E‑2 case must answer, derived from 9 FAM 402.9 and consular E‑visa guidance (e.g., E‑visa FAQs and supporting‑documentation pages). Designed for use as an intake and cover‑letter checklist.*

---

## 1. Treaty, nationality, and company ownership

1. **Treaty existence**  
   - Is there an E‑2 treaty between the United States and the investor’s nationality?[web:21][web:63]

2. **Investor nationality**  
   - Is the principal investor a national of that treaty country (not just a permanent resident of the U.S.)?[web:63]

3. **Enterprise nationality / ownership**  
   - Do nationals of the treaty country own **at least 50%** of the U.S. enterprise (counting through any holding companies)?[web:63][web:87]  
   - For layered or partnership structures, can you clearly document the chain of ownership down to natural persons and their nationalities?[web:63][web:87]

4. **Multiple or dual nationalities**  
   - If the enterprise is owned by people with different treaty nationalities or dual citizenship, which nationality is being designated as the **E‑visa nationality**, and do all intended E‑2 beneficiaries share that nationality?[web:63]

---

## 2. Investment, at‑risk capital, and timing

5. **Investment made or in process**  
   - Has the investor **already invested** or is clearly **in the process of investing** capital into the U.S. business (beyond preliminary intent)?[web:63][web:21]

6. **Irrevocably committed & at risk**  
   - Are the funds **irrevocably committed and at risk** in the commercial sense (e.g., spent on leases, build‑out, inventory, equipment, franchise fees, payroll, or placed in a binding escrow tied to business obligations), rather than just parked in a bank account?[web:63][web:85]

7. **Amount and proportionality (substantiality)**  
   - Is the invested (or committed) amount **substantial in a proportionality sense**, given the type of enterprise (start‑up vs. asset‑heavy business) and total cost of establishing or purchasing it?[web:63]

8. **Timing of investment steps**  
   - Are the key investment steps (wire transfers, executed contracts, expenditures) completed or contractually binding **before** the consular interview, in line with the “in the process of investing” standard?[web:63][web:28]

---

## 3. Real and operating enterprise

9. **For‑profit, commercial nature**  
   - Is the enterprise a **real, active, for‑profit commercial business**, not a non‑profit or purely passive investment vehicle?[web:63]

10. **Operational footprint**  
    - Can you show that the business is **real and operating or imminently operational** (e.g., business registration, premises lease, utilities, licenses, website, contracts, initial payroll or executed job offers)?[web:63][web:69][web:84]

11. **Not a shell or holding company**  
    - Is the company more than a shell holding stock, undeveloped land, or other passive assets, in line with 9 FAM’s “real and operating enterprise” test?[web:63]

---

## 4. Non‑marginality and business plan

12. **Non‑marginal enterprise**  
    - Does the business have the **present or future capacity** to generate significantly more than minimal living income for the investor and family, or otherwise make a significant economic contribution (e.g., employing U.S. workers)?[web:63][web:21]

13. **Business plan quality**  
    - Is there a **credible business plan** with market analysis, staffing plan, and multi‑year financial projections (typically 3–5 years) showing growth, job creation, and sufficient revenue to satisfy the non‑marginality standard?[web:84][web:86]

14. **Consistency between plan and evidence**  
    - Do the plan’s assumptions align with current evidence (e.g., leases, contracts, payroll, tax returns) and with industry norms, such that a consular officer could reasonably accept them?[web:84][web:63]

---

## 5. Source and path of funds

15. **Lawful source**  
    - Can the investor document that the invested capital comes from a **lawful source**, such as savings, salary, business profits, sale of property, inheritance, secured loans, or other “legitimate sources” as described in 9 FAM 402.9‑6(B)?[web:63]

16. **Complete path of funds**  
    - Is there a **clear, auditable path** from the original source into the U.S. enterprise, via bank statements, sale contracts, loan agreements, and (where relevant) exchange/wallet records, with no unexplained gaps?[web:63][web:87]

17. **Officer’s discretion for further documentation**  
    - Are you prepared for the consulate to request **additional documents as needed** (e.g., more detailed bank history, tax returns), given 9 FAM’s explicit grant of discretion to officers on source‑of‑funds evidence?[web:63]

---

## 6. Role, control, and “develop and direct”

18. **Investor vs. employee classification**  
    - Will the applicant be classified as a **treaty investor** (owner/developer) or as an **E‑2 employee** (executive/supervisory or specialized skills)?[web:63][web:21]

19. **Ownership and control**  
    - If applying as an investor, does the applicant own **at least 50% of the enterprise**, or otherwise have control through a managerial position or corporate device (e.g., board control, veto rights)?[web:63]

20. **Joint ventures and partnerships**  
    - If the business is a **two‑party 50/50 partnership or joint venture**, can you show that both parties have equal investment and **full management rights and responsibilities**, satisfying 9 FAM’s “negative control” theory?[web:63]  
    - If there are **more than two equal partners**, do you understand that no individual can establish control based on ownership alone and that E‑2 classification will likely be as employees, not investors?[web:63]

21. **Duties and qualifications**  
    - Does the applicant’s **CV and experience** credibly support the claimed role (developing and directing the business, or serving in an executive/supervisory/essential‑skills capacity) as required by 9 FAM 402.9‑6(F)?[web:63][web:69]

---

## 7. Nonimmigrant intent and immigration history

22. **Intent to depart**  
    - Can the applicant credibly express an **intent to depart the United States** when E‑2 status terminates, consistent with 9 FAM 402.9‑4(C) and consular E‑visa FAQs?[web:21][web:63]

23. **Ties abroad**  
    - What **ties outside the U.S.** (residence, family, employment, assets, long‑term plans) support this intent to depart?

24. **Prior U.S. immigration history**  
    - Does the applicant have prior **U.S. visas, entries, or status changes**, and can they document maintenance of status and compliance (I‑94s, I‑797 approvals, prior visas) where consulate instructions ask for this?[web:92]

---

## 8. Consular packet and post‑specific requirements

25. **Core forms and civil documents**  
    - Are all required forms completed and ready: DS‑160 confirmations, DS‑156E (where required), valid passports, photos, marriage and birth certificates for dependents, MRV fee receipts, and appointment confirmation pages?[web:79][web:82][web:84][web:86][web:87]

26. **Post‑specific packet format and page limits**  
    - Does the submission follow the **exact format and page limits** required by the consulate (e.g., Toronto’s “Supporting Documentation for New Cases,” Paris’s tabbed PDF structure, Italy’s single‑PDF tabs)?[web:87][web:84][web:82]

27. **Tax and performance evidence for existing businesses / renewals**  
    - For businesses with prior U.S. operations or for **renewals**, are recent U.S. corporate tax returns, P&L statements, W‑2/1099/941 forms, and ownership schedules (e.g., Schedule K) included, as required by consular renewal instructions?[web:96][web:91]
