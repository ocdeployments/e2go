# TAB B — Personal Documents Checklist

## Purpose

Tab B does not collect new answers — it generates a personalized checklist
of every personal document the applicant needs to physically gather for
their binder. The checklist is generated entirely from answers already
provided in Tabs A and Module 0.

**Output:** Personalized Tab B Document Checklist — every line references
the applicant by name, the specific document required, where to obtain it,
and which binder tab it belongs to.

## Checklist Items Generated

All items are generated conditionally based on prior answers:

| Document | Condition | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Passport biographical page (photocopy) | Always | Current valid passport |
| Two passport-style photographs | Always | Must meet U.S. visa photo standards |
| Canadian birth certificate | Always | Certified copy |
| DS-160 confirmation page (barcode page) | Always | Printed from ceac.state.gov after submission |
| DS-156E form | Principal applicant only | Completed and signed |
| MRV fee receipt | Always | From ais.usvisa-info.com/en-ca/niv |
| Appointment confirmation letter | Always | From scheduling portal |
| Marriage certificate | If married | Certified/notarized copy |
| Divorce certificate | If previously divorced | Certified/notarized copy |
| Legal name change documentation | If ever used other names | Court order or government document |
| Spouse's passport biographical page | If spouse applying | Copy only |
| Spouse's birth certificate | If spouse applying | Certified copy |
| Children's passport biographical pages | If children applying | Copy for each child |
| Children's birth certificates | If children applying | Certified copy for each |

**No questions are asked in Tab B.** All items are auto-generated.

# TAB C — Visa Category Confirmation Letter

## Purpose

Tab C generates a short formal letter confirming the applicant is applying
under the E-2 Treaty Investor classification, the treaty basis (Canada),
and the nature of the enterprise. This letter sits behind Tab C in the binder.

**Output:** One-page Visa Category Confirmation Letter — generated entirely
from prior answers. No new questions required.

## Content Generated

The letter is auto-generated from:
- Applicant full name (from QA-01)
- Canadian citizenship (from Q0-01)
- Business name (from QA-51)
- Business type and description (from QA-53)
- Investment amount (from QA-56)
- Ownership percentage (from QA-55)
- Processing path (consular vs. COS, from Q0-03)

**No questions are asked in Tab C.** Pure generation from prior data.

# TAB D — Cover Letter Generator

## Purpose

Tab D generates the cover letter — the most important narrative document
in the entire application. The cover letter is the consular officer's roadmap.
It must address every E-2 eligibility criterion directly, specifically, and
concisely using the applicant's own facts.

**Output:** Complete cover letter, structured in 7–8 paragraphs, each
addressing a specific E-2 criterion. Generated by the LLM from answers
across all tabs, then presented to the user for review and light editing.

## Questions

### QD-01
**Question:** How would you describe yourself professionally in 2–3 sentences?
**Type:** textarea
**Tooltip:** This feeds the opening paragraph of your cover letter. Describe
your background, experience, and what you bring to this business. Be specific —
mention your industry, years of experience, and key achievements.
**Example:** "I am a 15-year veteran of the food service industry, having
managed three successful restaurant locations across Ontario. I have direct
experience in operations, staff management, and franchise compliance."

### QD-02
**Question:** Why are you investing in this specific business?
**Type:** textarea
**Tooltip:** This feeds the investment motivation paragraph. Explain why this
business, why now, and why you are the right person to run it.

### QD-03
**Question:** What specific skills and experience do you bring that make you
qualified to run this business?
**Type:** textarea
**Tooltip:** Be specific. Reference your work history, any relevant certifications,
industry knowledge, and management experience. Generic answers produce weak
cover letters.

### QD-04
**Question:** What is your plan for the business in the first 12 months?
**Type:** textarea
**Tooltip:** Describe your first-year priorities: hiring, operations, revenue
targets, and how you will establish the business.

### QD-05
**Question:** Why do you intend to return to Canada when your E-2 status ends?
**Type:** textarea
**Tooltip:** This addresses the non-immigrant intent requirement. Describe
your ties to Canada — family, property, financial interests — and your
intention to depart the U.S. when the business ceases to operate or your
status ends.

### QD-06
**Question:** Is there anything unusual or complex about your case that the
cover letter should address directly?
**Type:** textarea
**Has N/A option:** Yes — "Nothing unusual"
**Tooltip:** Examples: prior visa refusal, unusual source of funds, a business
that changed from original plan, a gap in employment. Better to address it
proactively than leave it for the officer to question.

## Cover Letter Structure (LLM Generation Target)

| Paragraph | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Applicant introduction — name, nationality, classification being sought |
| 2 | The business — name, type, location, nature of operations |
| 3 | Investment — amount, source summary, at-risk confirmation |
| 4 | Substantiality — investment as % of total cost, proportionality argument |
| 5 | Non-marginality — revenue projections, hiring plan, economic contribution |
| 6 | Applicant qualifications — experience, skills, active role |
| 7 | Non-immigrant intent — Canadian ties, intent to depart |
| 8 | Closing — request for favorable consideration |

**Generation rule:** Every paragraph must reference the applicant's specific
data. No sentence can be generic enough to apply to any other applicant.

# TAB E — Ownership Structure

## Purpose

Tab E documents exactly how the business is owned and who controls it.
This tab produces the ownership narrative that supports Tab F (investment
proof) and Tab J (qualifications). For partnerships, this tab captures
both partners' ownership structure and operating agreement details.

**Output:** Ownership structure narrative + ownership diagram description
(for the org chart in Tab J).

## Questions — Solo Applicant

### QE-01
**Question:** What is the full legal name of your U.S. business entity?
**Type:** text
**Note:** Pre-filled from QA-51 — confirm

### QE-02
**Question:** What type of legal entity is the business?
**Type:** select
**Options:**
- LLC (Limited Liability Company)
- S-Corporation
- C-Corporation
- Sole Proprietorship
- Partnership
- I have not yet formed the entity
**Tooltip:** Most E-2 investors use an LLC. The entity type affects the
supporting documents required.

### QE-03
**Question:** In which U.S. state is the business registered?
**Type:** select (U.S. state list)

### QE-04
**Question:** What is the business EIN (Employer Identification Number)?
**Type:** text
**Has N/A option:** Yes — "Not yet obtained"
**Tooltip:** The EIN is the business's federal tax ID. It is obtained from
the IRS and is required for banking, hiring, and tax filing.

### QE-05
**Question:** When was the business entity formed or registered?
**Type:** date
**Has N/A option:** Yes — "Not yet formed"

### QE-06
**Question:** Do you own 100% of the business?
**Type:** select
**Options:** Yes / No — I share ownership
**Branch:** No → QE-06a
**QE-06a:** Who else has ownership? What percentage do they hold?
**Type:** text + number (percentage)
**Tooltip:** Any non-investor co-owner (silent partner, employee with equity,
etc.) must be documented here. Their ownership does not grant them E-2 status
unless they independently qualify.

### QE-07
**Question:** Is your ownership documented in a formal operating agreement
or shareholder agreement?
**Type:** select
**Options:** Yes / No / In progress
**Branch:** Yes → QE-07a
**QE-07a:** When was the operating agreement signed?
**Type:** date

## Questions — Partnership Applicant (Additional)

*Shown only if Q0-09 = "Two equal 50/50 owners"*

### QE-08
**Question:** What is your business partner's full legal name?
**Type:** text

### QE-09
**Question:** What is your business partner's ownership percentage?
**Type:** number
**Validation:** Must equal exactly 50
**Tooltip:** For both partners to qualify for E-2 status, ownership must
be exactly 50/50.

### QE-10
**Question:** Does the operating agreement specify both partners' management
roles and responsibilities independently?
**Type:** select
**Options:** Yes / No / In progress
**Tooltip:** Each partner must independently demonstrate they develop and
direct the business. The operating agreement is the primary document that
establishes this for both parties.

### QE-11
**Question:** Does your operating agreement include a clause giving each
partner the right to block major decisions (negative control)?
**Type:** select
**Options:** Yes / No / I am not sure
**Tooltip:** Negative control means neither partner can make major business
decisions without the other's agreement. This is the legal foundation
for a two-person E-2 partnership.
