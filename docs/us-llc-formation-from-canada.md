## Overview

A Canadian resident can form a U.S. Limited Liability Company (LLC) entirely
from Canada — no U.S. trip required, no U.S. address of your own required,
no U.S. Social Security Number required.

The entire process can be completed in approximately 1–3 weeks:
- State LLC formation: 1–5 business days (online)
- EIN from IRS: same day (phone) or 4 business days (fax)

This guide covers every step from choosing a state through receiving your EIN.

---

## Step 1 — Choose Your State

### The Three States Non-Residents Typically Use

**For an E-2 visa applicant:** You will almost certainly want to form your LLC
in the **state where your business will physically operate.** This is because
E-2 requires you to operate a real business at a real location — and forming
in Wyoming or Delaware while operating in Texas creates a "foreign LLC" in
Texas that must register and pay fees in Texas anyway. There is no benefit to
a separate state for most E-2 applications.

**If your business is not yet in a specific state** (pre-visa, pre-location
selection), the three most common choices for non-residents are:

| State | Formation Fee | Annual Fee | Privacy | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wyoming** | $100 | $60/year | High (no public member list) | Non-resident holding company, max privacy |
| **Delaware** | $110 | $300/year franchise tax | Moderate | Investor-backed, venture capital path |
| **Florida** | $125 | $138.75/year | Low | E-2 applicants operating in Florida |
| **Texas** | $308 | $0 (no annual report fee) | Low | E-2 applicants operating in Texas |

> Source: LLC University — Best State to Form an LLC in 2026 (llcuniversity.com, 2025)

**Wyoming** is often recommended for non-residents who need a U.S. entity
for banking and payment processing before their U.S. business location is
confirmed — low fees, high privacy, no annual report beyond a minimal filing.

> "If they aren't living in the US and have no physical 'nexus' (office/employees),
> Wyoming is the gold standard. Low fees, high privacy, and minimal reporting."
> — Reddit r/Business_Ideas (2026)

**Important for E-2:** Once you identify your business state, you will register
as a **foreign LLC** in that state (or dissolve the Wyoming LLC and re-form
in your operating state). Budget for this additional registration step.

## Step 2 — Choose a Name

Your LLC name must comply with the rules of your chosen state. General rules:
- Must include "Limited Liability Company," "LLC," or "L.L.C."
- Must not be the same as or confusingly similar to an existing registered
  name in that state
- May not include certain restricted words (Bank, Insurance, Corporation)
  without special approval

**Check name availability:**
- Wyoming: wyomingsos.gov (Secretary of State)
- Delaware: icis.corp.delaware.gov
- Florida: search.sunbiz.org
- Texas: mycpa.cpa.texas.gov/cbsc/index.html

Names can be checked online for free in all states before filing.

**Name reservation:** Most states allow you to reserve a name for 60–120 days
for a small fee ($10–$50) while you prepare your filing.

## Step 3 — Appoint a Registered Agent

Every U.S. LLC must maintain a **registered agent** — a person or entity
with a physical address in the formation state who is available during
business hours to receive legal documents, tax notices, and government
correspondence on behalf of the LLC.

**As a Canadian resident, you cannot be your own registered agent**
unless you have a physical address in the formation state.

**Registered agent service providers:**
Professional registered agent services handle this for $49–$300/year.
Well-known providers include:
- Northwest Registered Agent ($125/year — well-reviewed for small business)
- Registered Agents Inc.
- Incorp Services
- ZenBusiness (bundles formation + registered agent)

> ⚠️ Your registered agent's address will appear in public state records.
> For states where privacy is important (Wyoming), use a dedicated registered
> agent rather than a virtual mailbox service.

## Step 4 — File Articles of Organization

The **Articles of Organization** (sometimes called Certificate of Formation)
is the document that legally creates your LLC. It is filed with the state's
Secretary of State office.

### What the Articles Typically Contain

- LLC name
- Principal office address (can be your Canadian address in most states)
- Registered agent name and address
- Names of members or managers (varies by state — Wyoming does not require)
- Organizer name and signature
- Purpose of the LLC (typically "any lawful purpose")

### How to File

**Online (recommended):**
Most states offer online filing through the Secretary of State website.
Typical processing time: **1–5 business days.** Some states offer
expedited processing (same day or 24 hours) for an additional fee.

| State | Filing Website | Standard Processing | Expedite |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wyoming | wyomingsos.gov | 3–5 days | 1 day (+$100) |
| Delaware | corp.delaware.gov | 1–2 days | Same day (+$50–$1,000) |
| Florida | sunbiz.org | 3–5 days | Same day (+$30) |
| Texas | sos.texas.gov | 10–12 days | 2 days (+$25) |

**By mail:** All states accept mailed filings — slower (2–4 weeks).

**Through a registered agent or formation service:**
Many registered agent services offer to prepare and file the Articles
on your behalf as part of a bundle. Typical all-in cost: $50–$200
beyond the state fee.

## Step 5 — Draft an Operating Agreement

The Operating Agreement is an **internal document** — it is not filed
with the state but is legally critical. It defines:
- Ownership percentages (membership interests)
- How profits and losses are allocated
- How decisions are made (voting rights)
- What happens if a member wants to leave or sell
- Management structure (member-managed vs. manager-managed)

**Is it legally required?**
Most states do not require an Operating Agreement by law, but without one,
state default rules govern your LLC — which may not match your intentions.
For E-2 purposes, the Operating Agreement is a **required application document**
that the consular officer will review to confirm ownership structure and
management authority.

**For a single-member LLC:** A simple Operating Agreement can be prepared
from a template and self-executed. Ensure it clearly states:
- 100% membership interest held by you (your full legal name)
- Member-managed structure
- Your authority to execute contracts, open bank accounts, and make
  all business decisions

**For a multi-member LLC (50/50 partnership E-2 applications):**
A customized Operating Agreement prepared by an attorney is strongly
recommended. It must address role differentiation, deadlock resolution,
and exit provisions.

## Step 6 — Obtain an EIN from the IRS

The **Employer Identification Number (EIN)** is your LLC's federal tax
identification number, issued by the IRS. It is required to:
- Open a U.S. business bank account
- File federal tax returns
- Hire employees
- Apply for business licenses in most jurisdictions

> "Use Form SS-4 to apply for an employer identification number (EIN)."
> — IRS — About Form SS-4 (irs.gov)

### Method 1 — Phone (Fastest — Same Day)

International applicants (including Canadians) can obtain an EIN by phone:

> "International applicants may call **267-941-1099** (not a toll-free number)
> 6 a.m. to 11 p.m. (Eastern Time) Monday through Friday to obtain their EIN."
> — IRS Instructions for Form SS-4 (Rev. 12/2025)

**Before calling:**
1. Complete Form SS-4 in full (download at irs.gov/formss4)
2. Have the form in front of you — the IRS representative will ask
   questions directly from it
3. The caller must be the LLC member/owner or an authorized representative

**What you need ready on the call:**
- LLC legal name (exactly as filed with the state)
- State of formation
- Your full legal name (as responsible party)
- Your Canadian address
- Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) — used as foreign tax ID
  (note: you can provide your SIN; you do not need a U.S. SSN)
- Type of entity (LLC — treated as disregarded entity or partnership)
- Reason for applying (banking purposes / starting a business)
- Date the LLC was formed

**Result:** The IRS representative provides the EIN verbally during the call.
Write it down immediately. A CP 575 confirmation letter arrives by mail
(to your Canadian address) within 4–6 weeks.

> Source: Reddit r/llc (2025); Wise — EIN for Foreigners (wise.com, 2024);
> OFX — How to Get an EIN from Outside the U.S.

### Method 2 — Fax (4 Business Days)

Complete Form SS-4 and fax it to the IRS.

**International applicants fax number:**
> IRS Fax-TIN for international applicants: **855-215-1627**

> "Under the Fax-TIN program, you can receive your EIN by fax generally
> within 4 business days."
> — IRS Instructions for Form SS-4 (Rev. 12/2025)

**Critical:** Include a **return fax number** on the form. If you do not,
the IRS will mail the EIN instead, adding 4+ weeks.

Use an online fax service (eFax, mFax, Fax.Plus) — no fax machine required.
Upload the completed SS-4 PDF and fax it to the IRS number from your computer.

### Method 3 — Mail (4–8 Weeks)

Mail Form SS-4 to:
```
Internal Revenue Service
Attn: EIN International Operation
Cincinnati, OH 45999
```
Processing time: 4–8 weeks from mailing.

**Not recommended** unless phone and fax are unavailable.

### Method 4 — Online Application (U.S. Only)

The IRS online EIN application (irs.gov/ein) is only available to
applicants with a U.S. SSN or ITIN. **Canadian residents without a
U.S. SSN or ITIN cannot use the online application.** Use phone or fax.

## Step 8 — Register as a Foreign LLC in Your Operating State

If you formed your LLC in Wyoming or Delaware but your E-2 business will
physically operate in another state (e.g., Texas, Florida, California),
you must register as a **foreign LLC** in your operating state.

Filing a foreign LLC registration:
- Costs $50–$300 (state dependent)
- Requires a certificate of good standing from your formation state
- Requires a registered agent in the operating state
- Must be done **before** you begin business operations in that state

**For most E-2 applicants:** Form your LLC directly in your operating state
from the beginning. This avoids the foreign LLC registration step entirely.

## Step 9 — Compliance Checklist After Formation

| Task | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Draft and execute Operating Agreement | At formation | Required for E-2 and bank accounts |
| Obtain EIN | Day of or day after formation | Phone method = same day |
| Open business bank account | After EIN received | See banking guide |
| File initial report (if required by state) | Varies by state | Wyoming: not required; Delaware: yes; Florida: yes |
| Register in operating state (if foreign LLC) | Before operations | Certificate of good standing required |
| Apply for state/local business licenses | 4–12 weeks before opening | See `kb/licensing-by-state.md` |
| Open state tax accounts (sales tax, payroll) | Before first sale / hire | State Revenue Department |
