# e2go Document Generation — Follow-Up Conversation Specification
## Version 1.0 | June 2026

---

## Purpose

The Follow-Up Conversation fills Category B gaps identified by
the Analysis Engine. It runs between Module 3 completion and
document generation. It is not a form. It is a conversation —
one question at a time, generated specifically for this applicant,
designed to surface experience and motivation that structured
questions cannot capture.

---

## Stage 1 — Voice Sample and Writing Check

### Presentation to applicant

Show this screen before any questions:

---
"Before we build your documents, we want to make sure they
sound like you — not like a lawyer or a computer.

Take about ten minutes and write us two or three paragraphs
about why you chose this business and what it means to you
and your family. Write exactly like you would write a message
to a close friend. No formal language. Just you.

We use your writing to make your documents feel genuinely
yours — the way only you could have written them.

One important thing: please write this yourself, in your
own words. Do not use ChatGPT, Claude, or any other AI
writing tool. We check every writing sample and will ask
you to rewrite it if we detect AI-generated content.
The whole point is to capture your voice — not a machine's.

There are no wrong answers and no judgment on spelling
or grammar. The more naturally you write, the better
your documents will be."
---

Text area: minimum 150 words, maximum 800 words
Character counter shown
Submit button: "This is my own writing →"

### AI Detection Check on Submission

Run immediately on submit. Do not show loading state longer
than 3 seconds. Process in background.

Detection criteria:
- Perplexity score < threshold → likely AI
- Burstiness score < threshold → likely AI
- Pattern match against AI signature phrases → flag if 3+
- Vocabulary fingerprint → flag if matches AI distribution

#### If PASSES detection:
Proceed to voice profiling.
Extract content signals.
Show: "Thank you — we have what we need.
       Moving to your questions now."

#### If FAILS detection (first attempt):
Show warm retry message:

"It looks like this might have been written with some
AI assistance — and we completely understand.

But for this to work, we really do need your natural
writing. Your documents will be far more convincing
when they sound specifically like you.

Try again — imagine you are texting a friend who just
asked: 'So why are you actually doing this?' Two or
three paragraphs. Whatever comes out first."

[Try again →]

#### If FAILS detection (second attempt):
Switch to structured prompts:

"No problem at all — let us try something different.

Just answer these three questions below. Short answers
are completely fine — even a few sentences each.

1. What first made you think about this specific business?
2. What does a typical week look like for you right now?
3. What do you hope your life looks like three years
   after your business opens?

Write whatever comes to mind. We will take it from there."

Three separate text areas, minimum 50 words each.
These are designed to elicit natural conversational
responses that are nearly impossible to generate
convincingly with AI.

### Voice Profile Extraction

After clean sample confirmed, extract:

```
voice_profile = {
  avg_sentence_length: int (words),
  sentence_length_variance: float,
  vocabulary_complexity: "simple | accessible | professional | technical",
  formality_register: "casual | warm_formal | formal | legal",
  active_voice_ratio: float (0.0-1.0),
  first_person_frequency: "low | medium | high",
  argument_structure: "story_first | evidence_first | conclusion_first",
  emotional_tone: "warm | neutral | professional | passionate",
  specificity_tendency: "specific_details | general_statements | mixed",
  paragraph_length: "short | medium | long",
  punctuation_style: "minimal | standard | expressive"
}
```

### Content Signal Extraction

Simultaneously extract content:

```
content_signals = {
  motivation_story: str (what they said about why this business),
  personal_connection: str (any personal connection to the industry),
  family_context: str (what this move means for family),
  mentioned_experiences: [str] (any experience mentioned casually),
  mentioned_values: [str] (what they care about),
  concerns_expressed: [str] (any worries or hesitations mentioned),
  confidence_indicators: [str] (what they feel ready for)
}
```

## Stage 2 — Generated Follow-Up Questions

### Question Generation Logic

Based on the Case Brief gaps, generate questions in this priority order:

**Priority 1 — Experience gaps (if experience_score < STRONG)**

The engine selects from this question bank based on business type
and identified gaps. Questions are generated by AI — not scripted.
The templates below are guidance only. Each question must be
personalized to this applicant's specific situation.

CARE FRANCHISE (if experience_score < STRONG):

Template A — Family caregiving:
"You have chosen a home care business. Tell us about any experience
you have had caring for or supporting another person — in any
context. This could be a family member, a friend, through
volunteering, or anywhere else where you took responsibility
for someone's wellbeing."

Template B — If Template A response mentions caregiving:
"You mentioned [specific thing they said]. Can you tell us
more about [specific aspect]? How long did that last,
what did it involve day to day, and what did you learn
about managing care for someone who needed support?"

Template C — Community/volunteer:
"Have you been involved in any volunteer work, community
organizations, religious institutions, or similar groups
where you helped coordinate services or support for others?"

FOOD/HOSPITALITY FRANCHISE (if experience_score < STRONG):

Template A:
"Tell us about any experience you have had in customer-facing
work — even informally. This could be a part-time job, helping
in a family business, community events you organized, or
anything where you were responsible for serving or satisfying
other people."

EDUCATION FRANCHISE (if experience_score < STRONG):

Template A:
"Your business involves education and working with students.
Tell us about any experience you have had teaching, coaching,
mentoring, or working with young people — in any context."

ALL FRANCHISE TYPES — Management experience deep dive:

Template:
"In your work history, tell us about the largest team or group
of people you were ever responsible for — even informally.
What were you responsible for, how many people were involved,
and what decisions did you make?"

**Priority 2 — Motivation story (if motivation = null or thin)**

"Take a moment and tell us the real story behind this decision.
Not the investment case — what personally drew you to this
specific business? When did you first start thinking about it
and what made it feel right for you?"

**Priority 3 — Family context (if family_moving and intent_score < STRONG)**

"You mentioned [family situation from Tab L]. Tell us about
what staying connected to [home country] means to you —
professionally, personally, and for your family. What are
your plans for maintaining those connections?"

**Priority 4 — Foreign business involvement (always ask)**

"Have you ever been involved in running, managing, or owning
any kind of business — even informally, even partially, even
outside your home country? This includes helping in a family
business, having an ownership stake in any enterprise, or
managing any commercial activity."

**Priority 5 — Community and informal leadership**

"Outside of paid work, have you ever been in a leadership
position in any organization — religious institution,
community group, professional association, school board,
sports team, anything at all? What were you responsible for?"

### Question Presentation

Each question appears one at a time.
No numbered list. No "Question 3 of 7."
Just the question, a text area, and a Continue button.

After each response, the engine evaluates:
- Was the gap filled? → move to next gap
- Was the gap partially filled? → generate a follow-up probe
- Did the response reveal a new strength? → log to case brief
- Did the response reveal a new concern? → flag for framing

Maximum questions: 8 per session
Minimum required: questions covering Priority 1 and 2 gaps only

### Conversation Tone

Every question is preceded by a brief contextual sentence
that explains why we are asking. Never clinical. Never
interrogative. Always collaborative.

Example:
"The next question helps us present your qualifications
in the strongest possible light.

[question]"

Or:
"This is one of the questions that makes the biggest
difference to your application.

[question]"

---

## Stage 3 — Completion

When all Priority 1 and 2 gaps are filled, show:

"We have everything we need to build your documents.

Here is what stood out from our conversation:

[3-4 bullet points summarizing the strongest things
 they shared — their words, not legal language]

These will be woven into your application. Your documents
will be ready in a few minutes.

[Build my documents →]"

The bullet points serve two purposes:
1. They confirm to the client that we heard them
2. They are the first draft of the narrative bullets
   that appear in the cover letter

## Stage 4 — Data Storage

All follow-up conversation data is stored in the database:

```sql
table: applicant_voice_profile
  application_id
  writing_sample_text
  ai_detection_passed (boolean)
  ai_detection_score (float)
  voice_profile_json
  content_signals_json
  created_at

table: followup_responses
  application_id
  question_id
  question_text
  response_text
  gap_addressed
  strength_identified
  created_at
```

These records are inputs to every generation prompt.
They are never deleted — they are the foundation of the documents.
