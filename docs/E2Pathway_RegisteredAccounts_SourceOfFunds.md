# E-2 Source of Funds: Canadian Registered Accounts (RRSP, TFSA, LIRA, Pension, RESP)

> **Disclaimer:** This document is for educational preparation purposes only — not legal advice. Tax implications of registered account withdrawals are highly individual. All applicants using registered account funds should consult a cross-border CPA *before* withdrawal and a licensed U.S. immigration attorney before filing.

---

## Overview: The Governing Standard

9 FAM 402.9 does not itemize a document list specific to Canadian registered accounts. Instead, consular officers at Toronto apply the general E-2 source-of-funds standard: funds must be (1) **lawfully obtained**, (2) **traceable from origin to U.S. business account**, and (3) **irrevocably committed and at risk** in the enterprise.

Registered Canadian accounts (RRSP, TFSA, LIRA, pension, RESP) are **fully acceptable** E-2 source of funds — practitioner consensus confirms this — provided the complete paper trail is documented. The challenge is not eligibility; it is the *multi-step conversion process* that must be captured in documents.

> 📌 **Toronto-specific note:** The U.S. Consulate General in Toronto caps E-2 application packages at **50 pages**. Source-of-funds documentation for registered accounts can be voluminous. Practitioners recommend including the core chronological narrative and key statements in the submitted package, with full backup documentation prepared and available for interview.

## 1. RRSP Withdrawal — Documentation Requirements

### Why RRSP Funds Require Extra Documentation

An RRSP withdrawal adds two steps not present in a simple savings withdrawal: (1) a Canadian tax withholding event that must be explained, and (2) a conversion from CAD to USD. Both steps require documentation or the funds appear to "shrink" without explanation, raising officer concern.

### Required Paper Trail

| Step | Document Required | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| **1. Account history** | RRSP account statements (12 months prior to withdrawal) | Shows funds were legitimately accumulated; includes running balance |
| **2. Withdrawal confirmation** | RRSP redemption/withdrawal confirmation from financial institution | Shows amount redeemed, date, net amount after withholding |
| **3. Tax withholding** | **T4RSP slip** (Statement of RRSP Income) | CRA-issued; shows gross withdrawal, tax withheld — proves withholding is explained and lawful |
| **4. Receiving account** | Personal Canadian bank statement showing deposit of net proceeds | Links RRSP withdrawal to personal account |
| **5. CAD→USD conversion** | Forex/wire confirmation from bank or currency specialist (e.g., Wise, Knightsbridge FX) | Shows conversion rate, CAD amount in, USD amount out, date |
| **6. U.S. receiving account** | U.S. personal or business bank statement showing USD deposit | Closes the chain from RRSP to U.S. business |
| **7. Business deployment** | Invoices, receipts, contracts showing funds spent in the business | Proves funds are at risk |
| **8. Cover letter narrative** | Chronological source-of-funds explanation letter | Attorney-drafted; maps each step to exhibit tabs |

### Canadian Tax Slips for RRSP Withdrawals

- **T4RSP (Statement of RRSP Income):** Issued by the financial institution holding the RRSP. Reports gross amount withdrawn (Box 22) and income tax withheld (Box 30). This is the primary Canadian tax document for RRSP withdrawals and **should be included in the source-of-funds package**. It explains why the net deposit in the personal bank account is less than the gross amount redeemed.
- **NR4 slip:** Issued *instead of* a T4RSP if the withdrawal occurs **after the applicant has become a Canadian non-resident for tax purposes**. The NR4 reports the gross amount and the Part XIII non-resident withholding tax (typically 25%, reduced to 15% under the Canada-U.S. Tax Treaty for RRSP/RRIF withdrawals in most cases). If the applicant departed Canada before making the RRSP withdrawal, they will receive an NR4, not a T4RSP.

> ⚠️ **Officer concern — the withholding gap:** A common source-of-funds question arises when an applicant's RRSP redemption confirmation shows (e.g.) $150,000 CAD withdrawn, but only $112,500 CAD arrives in their bank account. Without the T4RSP or NR4 explaining the $37,500 withholding tax, the officer may flag the unexplained shortfall. Always include the T4RSP/NR4.

### Timing Consideration

If the RRSP is redeemed **while the applicant is still a Canadian resident**, they receive a T4RSP and the withholding rate is tiered (10% on withdrawals up to $5,000; 20% on $5,001–$15,000; 30% on amounts over $15,000 — Quebec rates differ). If redeemed **as a non-resident**, the NR4 applies at 25% (or 15% per treaty). A cross-border CPA must advise on the optimal timing.

## 2. TFSA Withdrawal — Documentation Requirements

### Key Difference from RRSP

A TFSA withdrawal is **not a taxable event in Canada** — no T4RSP, no NR4, and no withholding tax applies to a TFSA withdrawal made while the investor is still a **Canadian resident**. This actually makes the TFSA a cleaner source-of-funds story because there is no withholding gap to explain.

> ⚠️ **Non-resident TFSA complication:** If the applicant has already become a Canadian non-resident, contributions made to the TFSA as a non-resident attract a 1%/month penalty tax. Withdrawals as a non-resident are still tax-free in Canada. However, the **U.S. does not recognize the TFSA's tax-exempt status** — growth inside a TFSA is potentially taxable in the U.S. once the holder is a U.S. tax resident. A cross-border CPA must address this before withdrawal.

### Required Paper Trail

| Step | Document Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Account history** | TFSA account statements (12 months prior to withdrawal) | Shows accumulation and balance |
| **2. Withdrawal confirmation** | TFSA redemption/withdrawal confirmation | Amount and date |
| **3. Receiving account** | Canadian personal bank statement showing deposit | No tax slip required if Canadian resident at time of withdrawal |
| **4. CAD→USD conversion** | Forex confirmation | Rate, amounts, date |
| **5. U.S. account deposit** | U.S. bank statement | Closes the chain |
| **6. Business deployment** | Invoices, receipts, contracts | Proves at-risk investment |

**No T4RSP or NR4 is required for a TFSA withdrawal by a Canadian resident.** However, if the financial institution issues any confirmation statement, include it. The absence of a tax slip should be explained briefly in the cover letter narrative: *"The TFSA is a tax-free savings account; withdrawals do not attract Canadian withholding tax and are not reported on a CRA slip."*

## 3. LIRA / Pension Transfer — Documentation Requirements

### What is a LIRA?

A Locked-In Retirement Account (LIRA) holds pension funds that have been transferred out of an employer pension plan. Unlike an RRSP, LIRA funds are **locked in** — direct cash withdrawals are generally not permitted except under specific unlocking provisions governed by provincial pension legislation (e.g., Ontario: *Pension Benefits Act*; B.C.: *Pension Benefits Standards Act*; federal: OSFI rules).

### How LIRA Funds Can Be Accessed for E-2 Purposes

There are limited mechanisms to unlock LIRA funds:

| Unlocking Method | Condition | Governing Body |
|---|---|---|
| **Permanent departure from Canada** | Applicant is leaving Canada permanently | Provincial pension regulator (FSRA in Ontario, BCFSA in B.C., etc.) |
| **Small balance unlocking** | LIRA balance below provincial threshold | Provincial regulator |
| **Financial hardship** | Low income, rent arrears, medical expenses (specific criteria apply) | Provincial regulator |
| **Age-based conversion** | Convert to Life Income Fund (LIF) at age 55+ (varies by province) | Provincial regulator |
| **50% unlocking at conversion** | On conversion to LIF, up to 50% can be transferred to RRSP/RRIF (some provinces) | Provincial regulator |

**For E-2 purposes, the "permanent departure from Canada" unlocking route is the most directly relevant.** The investor who is leaving Canada to operate a U.S. business may qualify to unlock their LIRA on this basis.

### Required Paper Trail for LIRA Unlocking

| Step | Document Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Pension plan statement** | Original pension plan annual statements | Shows accumulation and employer contributions |
| **2. Commuted value transfer** | Transfer confirmation from pension plan to LIRA | Date, amount transferred |
| **3. LIRA statements** | 12 months of LIRA account statements | Shows the funds resting in LIRA |
| **4. Unlocking application** | Completed provincial unlocking form (e.g., FSRA Form 1 for Ontario) | Confirms legal basis for unlocking |
| **5. Unlocking confirmation** | Letter from financial institution or pension regulator confirming unlock approval | Evidence the unlock was lawfully authorized |
| **6. Withdrawal/transfer confirmation** | Confirmation of transfer from LIRA to personal account or RRSP (if conversion route used) | Amount, date |
| **7. T4RSP or RL-2** | If funds were transferred through an RRSP before withdrawal | Standard RRSP withdrawal chain then applies |
| **8. T4A or NR4** | Depending on tax residency at time of withdrawal | CRA-issued; explains withholding |
| **9. CAD→USD conversion** | Forex confirmation | Rate, amounts, date |
| **10. U.S. account deposit** | U.S. bank statement | Closes the chain |

> ⚠️ **Officer concern — LIRA complexity:** LIRA unlocking is a multi-step process involving provincial regulators that most U.S. consular officers are unfamiliar with. The source-of-funds cover letter **must explain what a LIRA is, why it was locked, and how it was lawfully unlocked**, with reference to the provincial legislation. Without this explanation, an officer may question why the funds were "blocked" and then suddenly accessible.

> 🔴 **Platform flag:** LIRA/pension situations are complex enough that an attorney referral flag should be triggered automatically whenever the applicant identifies pension or LIRA funds as part of their source of funds.

## 4. Canadian Tax Documents in the Source-of-Funds Package

| Tax Slip | When Issued | What It Shows | Include in Package? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **T4RSP** | RRSP withdrawal (Canadian resident) | Gross amount withdrawn, tax withheld | ✅ Always |
| **NR4** | RRSP/RRIF/LIRA withdrawal (non-resident) | Gross amount, Part XIII withholding | ✅ Always |
| **T4A** | Pension income, DPSP, certain registered plan distributions | Income and withholding | ✅ If applicable |
| **T4RIF** | RRIF withdrawal | Gross withdrawal, withholding | ✅ If applicable |
| **RL-2 (Quebec)** | Quebec equivalent of T4RSP/T4RIF | Same as T4RSP for Quebec residents | ✅ If Quebec resident |
| **T1 General (personal tax return)** | Annual CRA filing | Corroborates declared income and fund sources | ✅ Recommended (last 2 years) |
| **Notice of Assessment (NOA)** | CRA confirmation of T1 filing | Confirms tax compliance | ✅ Recommended |

**Why include tax returns?** A T4RSP slip alone shows the withdrawal. Personal T1 returns and NOAs show that the investor legitimately earned income in prior years that funded the RRSP contributions in the first place — completing the full chain of lawfulness all the way back to earned income.

## 5. U.S. Consulate Toronto Guidance & Officer Concerns

### Official Guidance

The U.S. Consulate General in Toronto has **not issued specific written guidance on registered account (RRSP/TFSA/LIRA) withdrawals** as E-2 source of funds. The Toronto consulate's published E-visa guidance directs applicants to submit documentation supporting lawful source and traceability but does not address registered accounts by name.

9 FAM 402.9 similarly contains no registered-account-specific provisions. The standard applied is the general lawful-source-and-traceability standard.

### Practitioner-Reported Officer Concerns

Based on practitioner reports and applicant accounts from the Toronto consulate:

1. **The withholding gap:** Officers flag unexplained discrepancies between redemption amounts and deposited amounts. Resolution: T4RSP/NR4 and a brief cover letter explanation.

2. **"Why did you cash out your retirement account?"** Officers sometimes question whether the investment is genuine when an applicant has liquidated a retirement account (implying financial pressure). Resolution: cover letter should proactively address this — *"The RRSP was liquidated as part of planned pre-departure financial restructuring to fund the U.S. business investment. The applicant maintains other Canadian financial assets including [list]."*

3. **Non-resident withholding confusion:** Officers unfamiliar with Canadian tax law may not understand why an NR4 shows a 25% withholding rate. Resolution: cover letter footnote briefly explaining Part XIII non-resident withholding.

4. **50-page cap:** Toronto's 50-page submission limit means extensive registered account statement histories must be curated. Practitioner consensus: include the 3 most recent full account statements, the withdrawal confirmation, and the T4RSP/NR4. Keep full history available for interview.

5. **LIRA/pension complexity:** Officers are most likely to issue a 221(g) request for additional evidence when LIRA or pension fund documentation lacks a clear narrative explaining the locked-in nature and the unlocking mechanism.

## 6. Known Denial and 221(g) Patterns Related to Registered Accounts

No published USCIS or State Department decisions specifically address Canadian registered account funds in E-2 adjudications. The following patterns are based on practitioner reports and applicant community accounts:

### 221(g) Requests (Administrative Processing / Additional Evidence)

- **Missing T4RSP/NR4:** Most commonly reported trigger. Officer cannot reconcile the withdrawal amount with the deposited amount without the tax slip.
- **Incomplete LIRA chain:** Unlock approval not documented; officer cannot verify the unlock was lawful.
- **Currency conversion gap:** CAD withdrawn from RRSP, USD appears in U.S. account — no forex conversion record linking the two.
- **RRSP contributions not traced:** Officer questions whether the RRSP contributions themselves were from lawful earned income. Resolution: include 2 years of T1 returns and NOAs.

### Outright Denial (Less Common, Registered Account Context)

- Rarely attributed specifically to registered account fund source. More commonly, a denial occurs when registered account funds are *combined* with other unexplained sources (e.g., cash deposits) and the overall package lacks credibility.
- One practitioner-reported pattern: investor liquidated RRSP and TFSA simultaneously, total CAD proceeds exceeded declared income history, no T1 returns included — officer denied on lawful source grounds.

> ⚠️ **Official sources are silent on registered-account-specific denial cases.** The above reflects practitioner consensus, not published adjudication records.

## 7. Recommended Chronological Paper Trail: RRSP to U.S. Business Account

The following is the recommended step-by-step documentary chain for an RRSP-sourced E-2 investment. This structure mirrors the "source-of-funds chronology" template format used in a well-organized E-2 package.

```
STEP 1 — RRSP ACCUMULATION (Historical Foundation)
  Documents:
  ├── Personal T1 General returns (2–3 years) showing employment/business income
  ├── CRA Notices of Assessment (2–3 years)
  ├── RRSP contribution receipts (optional but strengthening)
  └── RRSP account statements (12 months pre-withdrawal)

STEP 2 — RRSP WITHDRAWAL
  Documents:
  ├── RRSP redemption/withdrawal request form (if available from institution)
  ├── RRSP withdrawal confirmation from financial institution
  │     (shows gross amount, withholding tax deducted, net amount paid out)
  └── T4RSP slip (Statement of RRSP Income) — or NR4 if non-resident

STEP 3 — NET PROCEEDS TO CANADIAN PERSONAL ACCOUNT
  Documents:
  └── Canadian personal bank statement showing deposit of net RRSP proceeds
        (date, amount must match withdrawal confirmation net of withholding)

STEP 4 — ADDITIONAL FUNDS CONSOLIDATION (if applicable)
  Documents:
  ├── TFSA withdrawal confirmation + bank deposit statement (if TFSA also used)
  ├── Other asset sale records (if additional funds added)
  └── Consolidated Canadian personal account statement showing full investment pool

STEP 5 — CAD → USD CURRENCY CONVERSION
  Documents:
  ├── Currency conversion/wire confirmation from bank or FX specialist
  │     (shows: CAD amount sent, exchange rate applied, USD amount received, date)
  └── Bank statement showing CAD debit and USD credit (or two statements if cross-institution)

STEP 6 — USD TO U.S. PERSONAL OR BUSINESS ACCOUNT
  Documents:
  ├── Wire transfer receipt (SWIFT confirmation)
  └── U.S. bank statement showing USD deposit

STEP 7 — INVESTMENT DEPLOYMENT (Funds At Risk)
  Documents:
  ├── Franchise fee payment receipt / confirmation
  ├── Lease deposit and signed lease agreement
  ├── Equipment purchase invoices
  ├── Legal/professional fees (attorney, accountant)
  ├── Business bank account opening statement
  └── Any other startup expenditure receipts

COVER LETTER NARRATIVE
  └── One-page chronological summary mapping each step above to
      the corresponding exhibit tab, explaining T4RSP withholding,
      conversion rate, and business deployment
```

## 8. RESP Funds (Additional Note)

A **Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP)** is generally not a suitable E-2 source of funds because:

- RESP funds are intended for a beneficiary's (child's) education expenses
- Non-educational withdrawals (called "AIP" — Accumulated Income Payments) are subject to the subscriber's tax rate **plus a 20% penalty tax** (12% in Quebec)
- The grant portions (CESG) must be repaid to the government on non-educational withdrawal

**Practitioner consensus:** RESP funds should not be used as E-2 investment capital unless the subscriber has maximized other sources. If used, the AIP tax slip, penalty documentation, and grant repayment must all be documented. An attorney referral flag is appropriate.

- **All paths:** Prompt for forex conversion documentation; trigger CADUSD converter module

### Compliance Calendar Entries (Registered Account Path)

- T-6 months: Consult cross-border CPA re: optimal withdrawal timing (resident vs. non-resident)
- T-5 months: Initiate RRSP/TFSA redemption if not yet done
- T-4 months: Confirm T4RSP/NR4 slip received from financial institution (issued by end of February following tax year)
- T-3 months: Complete CAD→USD conversion; confirm wire received in U.S. account
- T-2 months: Confirm all investment receipts collected; source-of-funds chronology complete
- Interview: Bring originals of all documents listed above
