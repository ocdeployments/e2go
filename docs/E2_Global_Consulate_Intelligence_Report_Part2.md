## U.S. Embassy London (United Kingdom)

### Section 1 — Application Structure
- **Submission method:** Digital upload via the U.S. Embassy London online portal (not physical binder)
- **File size limit:** Maximum **20MB** total per submission 🗓️
- **Tab structure:** Standard E-2 package: DS-160, MRV fee receipt, passport photos, passport copy, business plan, investment evidence, source of funds, organizational documents, lease/purchase agreements, cover letter
- **Additional documents:** UK-specific proof of lawful business registration (Companies House extract recommended); bank statements typically required for last 12 months
- **Language:** English only — no translation required for UK nationals
- **Cover letter format:** Addressed to "Nonimmigrant Visa Unit, U.S. Embassy London"; should summarize treaty basis, investment amount, business description, and job creation plan

### Section 2 — Interview Process
- **Interview required:** Yes, in-person interview at the U.S. Embassy, 33 Nine Elms Lane, London SW11 7US
- **Typical duration:** 10–20 minutes 🔄
- **Interview tone:** Generally professional and businesslike; reported as less adversarial than some posts (community reported)
- **Common questions:** Describe your business; how did you fund the investment; how many employees will you hire; what is your role day-to-day; where is the business located; what is your exit plan if the business fails
- **Attorneys permitted:** Attorneys may accompany applicants to the embassy but are generally not permitted inside the interview room 🔄 Verify current policy
- **Interpreters:** Not typically required for UK nationals (English-language post)

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** 🔄 2–4 months as of 2025–2026 (community reported; verify via ustraveldocs.com)
- **Interview to visa issuance:** Typically 5–10 business days if approved without administrative processing
- **Expedited appointments:** Available in limited circumstances; emergency/urgent criteria apply; contact the embassy directly
- **Visa validity issued:** Typically **5 years** for UK nationals 🗓️
- **Duration of status on entry:** Up to 2 years per admission; renewable indefinitely by re-entry or extension

### Section 4 — Known Consulate-Specific Requirements
- **20MB file size cap** is a hard London-specific limit — large PDF packages must be compressed 🗓️
- Cover letters should be concise and structured; verbose applications are not favored
- Business plan should demonstrate genuine job creation for U.S. workers
- **Franchise applications:** London processes a high volume of U.S. franchise E-2s for UK nationals; franchise disclosure documents (FDD) expected
- Financial documents: no apostille requirement for UK-sourced documents; foreign-sourced funds may require certified translation

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds
- No official minimum; informal threshold commonly cited at **$100,000+** for low-risk industries
- London officers known to scrutinize investments below $80,000 heavily (community reported)
- Service businesses and consulting firms receive additional scrutiny regarding "marginality"
- Investment must be "at risk" — escrow arrangements accepted if properly structured

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns
- **Most common denial reasons:** Marginality (business unlikely to generate more than a living for investor); insufficient investment; lack of genuine managerial role
- **221(g) patterns:** Administrative processing not uncommon for first-time applicants; often requests additional financial documentation or business plan revisions 🔄
- **High-scrutiny business types:** Solo consulting/coaching businesses; online-only businesses with no physical U.S. presence; businesses with very low startup costs

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal / ustraveldocs.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD (E visa category) 🗓️ Annual Review Required
- **Biometrics:** Collected at the embassy on interview day
- **DS-160 notes:** Select "London" as the consular post; ensure DS-160 confirmation barcode matches appointment record
- **Known backlogs:** Moderate; 2–4 month waits typical in 2025–2026 🗓️

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- London has a well-developed E-2 immigration attorney community; many UK-based U.S. immigration law firms specialize in E-2
- Strong franchise broker network in the UK; many U.S. franchise brands actively market E-2 pathways to UK nationals
- E-2 is well understood in the UK — marketed prominently by immigration consultants and franchise expos
- **Cultural note:** UK applicants tend to be well-prepared and document-savvy; interview preparation should emphasize "hands-on management" role to distinguish from passive investment

## U.S. Consulate Toronto (Canada)

### Section 1 — Application Structure
- **Submission method:** Physical document package submitted at interview; some pre-submission via mail depending on instructions 🔄 Verify current intake method
- **Tab structure:** Standard E-2 tabs: DS-160 confirmation, MRV fee receipt, passport + photos, cover letter, business plan, organizational documents, investment evidence, source of funds, leases, licenses, and financial projections
- **Additional documents (Canada-specific):** As of 2025, **all family members (spouse and children) must attend the E-2 interview** — a significant Canada-specific requirement 🗓️
- **Language:** English or French accepted; English strongly preferred for all business documentation
- **Physical binder:** Physical organized tabbed binder is the standard format for Toronto submissions; use labeled dividers

### Section 2 — Interview Process
- **Interview required:** Yes, mandatory in person at U.S. Consulate General Toronto, 360 University Avenue
- **Family attendance:** New 2025 requirement — spouse and dependent children must be present at interview 🗓️
- **Typical duration:** 15–30 minutes 🔄
- **Interview tone:** Professional; Toronto officers are known for detailed, methodical questioning (community reported)
- **Common questions:** How did you fund the investment? What is your day-to-day role? How many U.S. citizens or permanent residents will you hire? Walk me through your business plan financials. Have you operated this business type before?
- **Attorneys permitted:** 🔄 Generally not inside the interview room; confirm with consulate
- **Interpreters:** Not typically required for Canadian nationals

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** ~4 months as of Q4 2025 (Frear Law verified attorney data) 🗓️
- **Interview to visa issuance:** 7–10 business days standard; may extend if administrative processing triggered
- **Expedited appointments:** Available; criteria include urgent business need; apply through ustraveldocs.com
- **Visa validity issued:** Typically **5 years** for Canadian nationals 🗓️
- **Duration of status on entry:** Up to 2 years per admission; renewable

### Section 4 — Known Consulate-Specific Requirements
- **All family at interview** is the most critical Toronto-specific requirement — failure to bring family members can result in rescheduling
- Tabbed physical binder expected; disorganized submissions reflect poorly
- Cover letter should address treaty basis explicitly (CUSMA/USMCA for Canada)
- Financial projections: 3–5 year pro forma expected; must show path to profitability beyond marginal income
- 🔄 Verify whether digital upload option has been introduced since Q4 2025

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds
- Informal threshold: **$100,000+** commonly expected; higher for capital-intensive industries
- Canadian dollar investments must be clearly converted to USD in the application
- Officers evaluate investment proportionality relative to business type and total enterprise value
- Franchise purchases common; full FDD and franchise agreement required

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns
- **Most common denials:** Marginality; passive investment (applicant not managing day-to-day); insufficient source of funds documentation
- **221(g) patterns:** Requests for additional financial records, updated business plan, or proof of active business operations
- **High-scrutiny types:** Online businesses; real estate investment (unless substantial and active management demonstrated); single-employee operations

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal via ustraveldocs.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD 🗓️
- **Biometrics:** Collected at consulate on interview day
- **DS-160:** Select "Toronto" as post; barcode must match appointment
- **Known backlogs:** ~4 months Q4 2025; check current availability at ustraveldocs.com 🗓️

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- Strong Canadian E-2 attorney community in Toronto, Vancouver, and Calgary
- Active Canadian investor interest in U.S. franchises; many franchise brokers in Canada explicitly market E-2 visa pathways
- E-2 is well understood and frequently discussed in Canadian immigrant entrepreneur communities
- **Cultural note:** Canadian applicants often have strong business documentation habits; emphasis should be on demonstrating active U.S.-based management role, not passive ownership

## U.S. Embassy Mexico City / Consulates (Mexico)

### Section 1 — Application Structure
- **Posts processing E-2:** Mexico City (Embassy), Monterrey, Guadalajara, Tijuana, Ciudad Juárez, Hermosillo, Mérida, Matamoros, Nogales, Nuevo Laredo
- **Highest E-2 volume posts:** Monterrey and Mexico City (community reported)
- **Submission method:** CGI Federal / ustraveldocs.com; physical document package typically brought to interview
- **Tab structure:** Standard E-2 package; Mexican corporate documents (acta constitutiva, RFC) with certified English translation
- **Language:** All Spanish-language documents require certified English translation
- **Additional documents:** Mexican tax records (SAT constancia), bank statements

### Section 2 — Interview Process
- **Interview required:** Yes, in person
- **Typical duration:** 10–30 minutes 🔄
- **Interview tone:** Variable by post; Monterrey known for thorough questioning; border posts (Tijuana, Juárez) have high-volume environments (community reported)
- **Common questions:** Standard E-2 questions; business description, source of funds, management role, hiring plan
- **Interpreters:** Spanish-speaking staff at all Mexico posts
- **Attorneys permitted:** 🔄 Generally not inside interview room

### Section 3 — Processing Times
- **Appointment wait:** 🔄 3–6 months estimated; varies significantly by post
- **Interview to visa issuance:** 5–10 business days standard; can extend for administrative processing
- **Visa validity issued:** Typically **5 years** for Mexican nationals (subject to reciprocity) 🗓️
- **Duration of status:** Up to 2 years per admission

### Section 4 — Known Consulate-Specific Requirements
- Acta constitutiva (Mexican articles of incorporation) required for Mexican-entity investments with certified translation
- RFC (Mexican tax ID) documentation for source of funds
- Each Mexico consular post may have slightly different intake logistics — verify specific post instructions
- 🗓️ Annual Review Required for each sub-post's specific procedures

### Section 5 — Investment Thresholds
- Informal threshold: **$100,000+**; Mexican investors span a wide range from franchise buyers ($80K–$200K) to larger business acquisitions
- Restaurant, food service, retail, and logistics-adjacent businesses common

### Section 6 — Denial Patterns
- **Common denials:** Marginality; source of funds documentation gaps; passive investment
- **221(g):** Administrative processing for additional financial documentation fairly common 🔄
- **High-scrutiny:** Cash-intensive businesses; real estate passive structures

### Section 7 — Practical Logistics
- **Appointment system:** CGI Federal via ustraveldocs.com
- **MRV fee:** $205 USD 🗓️
- **Biometrics:** Collected at each post
- **DS-160:** Select the specific Mexico consular post applicant is attending
- **Backlogs:** 3–6 months; varies by post 🗓️

### Section 8 — Local Market Context
- Extremely active E-2 attorney community across Mexico; particularly strong in Mexico City and Monterrey
- U.S. franchise brands heavily marketed to Mexican investors as E-2 visa vehicles
- Binational business associations (AmCham Mexico) are active E-2 education resources
- **Cultural note:** Mexican applicants often have strong business networks in both countries; interview prep should emphasize the genuine managerial role and active day-to-day involvement
