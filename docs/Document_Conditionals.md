## Purpose

This file defines exactly which documents are generated for each
applicant based on their specific situation. No document is generated
unless it is genuinely required. The dashboard shows only documents
relevant to this applicant — never a generic fixed list.

---

## The Two Generation Batches

### Batch 1 — Generated Immediately After Payment
Requires only personal information. No business formation needed.
Generated as soon as Module 3 personal tabs are complete.
(Tabs A, B, J, and the personal section of Tab F)

| Document | Always / Conditional |
|---|---|
| Principal Applicant Declaration | Always |
| Qualifications Summary | Always |
| Personal Net Worth Statement | Always |
| Dependent Spouse Declaration | If spouse traveling |

User sees Batch 1 documents immediately. This demonstrates value
at the point of payment before the long business formation wait.

### Batch 2 — Generated as Business Formation Completes
Each document has specific prerequisites confirmed via the
three-phase checklist. Generated per-document as prerequisites
are met. Cover Letter is always the last document generated.

All remaining documents listed below are Batch 2.

### CONDITIONALLY GENERATED — Per Person

#### Dependent Spouse
**Trigger:** family_type = couple OR family
**Documents generated:**
- Dependent Spouse Declaration
- Spouse DS-160 Reference
- Spouse Qualifications Summary (only if professional background
  is relevant to the application — determined by Tab L answers)

#### Each Child Traveling
**Trigger:** number_of_children_traveling > 0
**Documents generated per child:**
- Child Declaration (named individually)
- Child DS-160 Reference (named individually)

Children remaining in Canada: no documents generated.

---

### CONDITIONALLY GENERATED — Per Fund Source

#### Canadian Property in Fund Source
**Trigger:** Tab F answer — Canadian property is a source of
investment funds = yes
**Documents generated:**
- Property Portfolio Summary
**Content:** Summary of Canadian properties, equity values,
mortgage balances, and how proceeds feed the investment.
**Note:** Property appraisals or tax assessments are user uploads —
not generated by e2go. The summary references them.

#### Investment Accounts in Fund Source
**Trigger:** Tab F answer — RRSP, TFSA, or brokerage accounts
are a source of investment funds = yes
**Documents generated:**
- Investment Portfolio Summary
**Content:** Summary of Canadian investment accounts, values,
and how withdrawals feed the investment.
**Note:** Account statements are user uploads — not generated.

### CONDITIONALLY GENERATED — Partnership Applications

**Trigger:** application_type = partnership

Additional documents generated for Partner 2:
- Partner 2 Applicant Declaration
- Partner 2 Qualifications Summary
- Partner 2 DS-160 Reference
- Partner 2 Source of Funds Statement
- Partner 2 Fund Flow Chronology
- Partner 2 Net Worth Statement
- Partner 2 Property Portfolio (if Partner 2 has property in fund source)
- Partner 2 Investment Portfolio (if Partner 2 has accounts in fund source)

Shared documents — generated once for both partners:
- Business Plan
- Substantiality Memorandum
- Marginality Rebuttal
- Operating Agreement
- Cover Letter (references both partners)

**⚠️ Partnership submission format: UNCONFIRMED**
Whether both partners submit one combined binder or two separate
packages has not been confirmed from official sources.
Build both modes. User confirms format before generation proceeds.
Contact EVisaCanada@state.gov to confirm.
Do not publish page budget numbers for partnerships until confirmed.

### USER UPLOADS — Never Generated by e2go

These documents are produced by the registered agent or attorney
who forms the LLC. e2go collects them as uploads, not generated docs.

| Document | Source |
|---|---|
| Membership Interest Ledger | Registered agent |
| Membership Interest Certificates | Registered agent |
| Unanimous Written Consent of Members | Registered agent |

The app provides the user with instructions on how to obtain
each of these from their registered agent or attorney.
These are listed on the checklist as user upload items.
The checklist cannot be marked complete without these uploaded.

## Operating Agreement Special Case

**If LLC is already formed by attorney:**
- User uploads the existing attorney-drafted Operating Agreement
- e2go does NOT generate an Operating Agreement
- The upload replaces the generated document in the package

**If LLC is not yet formed:**
- e2go generates an Operating Agreement template
- Clearly marked as a DRAFT for attorney review
- Disclaimer: "This document is a preparation draft.
  It must be reviewed and executed by a qualified attorney
  before submission."

## Generation Gate Logic

Before any Batch 2 document can be generated, the app checks
the three-phase checklist for that document's specific prerequisites.

### Source of Funds Statement requires:
- U.S. business bank account: confirmed ✅
- Investment funds transferred: confirmed ✅
- Wire transfer confirmation: obtained ✅
- Franchise fee or purchase payment: confirmed ✅

### Business Plan requires:
- LLC formed: confirmed ✅
- EIN obtained: confirmed ✅
- Franchise agreement or purchase agreement executed: confirmed ✅
- Target state and city confirmed: yes ✅
- Financial projections answers complete: yes ✅

### Substantiality Memorandum requires:
- Final investment amount confirmed: yes ✅
- Total business cost confirmed: yes ✅
- Investment as percentage of total cost calculable: yes ✅

### Operating Agreement requires:
- LLC formed: confirmed ✅
- Member names and ownership percentages confirmed: yes ✅
- Management rights structure confirmed: yes ✅

### Cover Letter requires:
- ALL other documents generated and quality-gate passed ✅
- Tab index auto-generated from completed documents ✅

## Answer Change Handling

If a user changes an answer after a document has been generated:

1. The affected document is flagged: "This document may need
   to be regenerated based on updated answers."
2. The consistency checker runs across all documents.
3. Any inconsistencies are flagged before the user proceeds.
4. User must confirm regeneration of affected documents.
5. Regenerated documents go through quality gate again.
6. Cover Letter regenerated last if any other document changes.

Never silently update a document. Always confirm with user first.

- Add to risk flags shown in dashboard throughout application

## Banking Sequence — Required in Phase 1 Checklist

Research confirms (E2_Answers_Part5_GreenCard_Tax.md, E2_Answers_Part6):
The banking sequence is not just operational — it is evidence for the application.
Timing of fund movements feeds directly into the Fund Flow Chronology.

Add to Phase 1 checklist in this specific order:

☐ Obtain EIN from IRS (irs.gov — free)
☐ Open U.S. business bank account (after EIN)
   Recommended: TD Bank or RBC Bank USA for Canadians
   (both offer cross-border banking specifically for Canadian businesses)
☐ Wire investment funds to U.S. business account
   Consider using a currency transfer specialist for large amounts:
   Wise, OFX, or Knightsbridge Foreign Exchange typically offer better
   rates than Canadian bank wire transfers (show projected savings comparison)
☐ Keep Canadian bank accounts open through interview
   DO NOT close Canadian accounts before visa approval
   Open Canadian accounts = evidence of Canadian ties = non-immigrant intent
☐ Update Canadian financial institutions after visa approval and relocation

## Frankfurt Exception — Document Compression

**Trigger:** consulate == Frankfurt (Germany)
**Hard limit:** 30 pages maximum, 5MB maximum

When Frankfurt is selected:
- Business Plan: generate executive summary version only
- All per-document page targets compress to 60% of standard
- Cover Letter: 3 pages maximum (not 5)
- Source of Funds: 2 pages maximum (not 3)
- Net Worth Statement: 1 page maximum (not 2)
- Business Plan: 8 pages maximum executive summary (not 18)
- Total target: 24 pages — 6 page buffer for 30-page limit

Display warning throughout document generation:
"Your application is being formatted for U.S. Consulate Frankfurt's
30-page limit. All required content is included in compressed format."
