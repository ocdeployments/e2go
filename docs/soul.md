## Why This Exists

The founder of E2Pathway went through the E-2 visa process personally.

It started with an immigration agent who led the process astray. Then came an immigration lawyer — $7,000 to $8,000 — who ultimately submitted documents that were, in large part, drafts the founder had already created independently using available tools.

That experience revealed something important: the knowledge exists. The process is knowable. The documents are producible. What is missing is a structured, honest, guided system that walks a person through it — one question at a time — without charging them lawyer rates for work they are largely capable of doing themselves.

There are tens of thousands of Canadians asking the same questions every year. They are searching forums, hiring agents, calling lawyers, and paying enormous fees for a process that has never been properly organized into a self-serve product. E2Pathway exists to fix that.

---

## What We Are

E2Pathway is a preparation and document drafting tool for Canadians applying for the U.S. E-2 Treaty Investor visa.

We help users:
- Understand whether they are eligible
- Select the right business
- Answer the right questions
- Prepare draft documents suitable for submission
- Organize their application binder to Toronto consulate standards
- Prepare for their interview

That is what we are. Nothing more. Nothing less.

---

## What We Are Not

We are not an immigration lawyer.
We are not a legal service.
We are not a substitute for legal advice.
We do not guarantee any outcome.
We do not review, certify, or approve applications.

The documents E2Pathway generates are **drafts**. They are built from the user's own answers, organized to consulate standards, and written in the appropriate format and language. Whether those drafts are suitable for submission is a decision that belongs to the user — and in complex cases, to a qualified immigration attorney.

This distinction is not fine print. It is the foundation of everything we do. It must appear clearly, consistently, and honestly across every surface of the product.

## Who We Are Building For

A Canadian. Likely 35–55 years old. Making one of the biggest financial and life decisions they have ever made.

They are not unsophisticated. They are intelligent, capable people who are navigating an unfamiliar system for the first time. They have probably spent months researching before they found us. They have read Reddit threads, watched YouTube videos, possibly hired an agent who gave them wrong information, and they still are not sure they understand the process correctly.

They are anxious — not because they are weak, but because the stakes are real. Their money is on the line. Their family's future in the United States depends on getting this right.

Our job is to systematically replace that anxiety with clarity — one question, one answer, one confirmed step at a time.

By the time they walk into the Toronto consulate, they should feel **prepared** — not just hopeful.

## The Hard Rules

These are not guidelines. They are constraints. They do not bend.

**1. We never take money from someone we cannot help.**
The eligibility quiz runs before any payment is taken. If a user's situation is high-risk — prior visa denial, criminal history, unusual business structure, immigration complications — the app identifies this in Module 0, explains honestly why the situation is complex, and directs them to a qualified immigration attorney. We do not charge them. We do not proceed as if the risk does not exist.

**2. We respect the user's autonomy.**
If a high-risk user understands the warning and chooses to continue, we allow it. We present the risk clearly. We require an explicit acknowledgment — a signed consent moment — before proceeding. We document that they were warned. Then we help them as best we can. We are never paternalistic. We are always honest.

**3. We never generate content from data we do not have.**
Every sentence in every generated document comes from an answer the user explicitly provided. We do not infer. We do not fill gaps with plausible-sounding language. If a section cannot be completed because the user has not provided the information, it remains incomplete until they do.

**4. We never lose a user's progress.**
Auto-save after every answer. No exceptions. The user should be able to close the browser mid-session, open it three days later on a different device, and find everything exactly where they left it.

**5. There are no dead ends.**
Every error has a next step. Every warning has an explanation. Every gap has a resolution path. If the app cannot help, it says so clearly and points the user somewhere that can.

**6. The PDF export works. Every time.**
The moment of export is the moment of highest anxiety. The product must not fail at this moment under any circumstance. Load test for the night before the Toronto interview.

## Document Integrity

Every document this app generates is a professional submission to a U.S. consulate officer. It must read as if it was written by a master editor who is simultaneously an immigration expert, a professional document drafter, and a forensic fact-checker.

The standards are non-negotiable:

- **No repetition.** No sentence appears twice. No piece of information is restated in a different tab because it already appeared in another. The consulate officer reads the entire package — they notice redundancy and it signals a lack of editorial control.
- **No filler.** Every word is there because it must be there. Introductory paragraphs that restate what the cover letter already said are cut. Boilerplate language that could apply to any applicant is cut.
- **No inference.** Nothing is written that the user did not explicitly provide. If a section cannot be completed from the user's answers, it remains incomplete until they provide the information.
- **Precision over volume.** The 50-page limit is not a target — it is a ceiling. A tight, precise, well-organized package from a well-prepared applicant outperforms a bloated one every time.
- **One story, told once, told well.** The application tells a single coherent narrative: who this investor is, where their money came from, what business they are building, why it will succeed, and why they will return to Canada when their status ends. Every tab supports that story. No tab contradicts another.

The AI generation layer must be instructed to write at this standard. The prompt architecture must enforce it. The document review checklist must check for it. It is not a style preference — it is the product's core quality commitment.

## Source Integrity

Every factual claim in this app — in tooltips, warning messages, educational content, and generated documents — must be traceable to an official government source.

| Tier | Source | Trust Level |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | U.S. Embassy Toronto (ca.usembassy.gov) | Authoritative — use as-is |
| 2 | State Department Foreign Affairs Manual (fam.state.gov) | Authoritative — use as-is |
| 3 | USCIS.gov official guidance | Authoritative — use as-is |
| 4 | Travel.state.gov | Authoritative — use as-is |
| 5 | Immigration law firm websites, blogs, video | Reference only — must be cross-verified against Tier 1–4 |
| 6 | Reddit, Facebook groups, forums | Never used as factual basis |

If a fact cannot be traced to Tier 1–4, it is either verified or removed. This applies to every tooltip, every warning, every educational paragraph, and every piece of generated content.

When official guidance is silent on a specific question, the app says so plainly and directs the user to verify with the U.S. Embassy Toronto or a qualified immigration attorney. We do not fill silence with plausible-sounding content.

## On Refusal

If a user prepares their full application through E2Pathway, attends their Toronto interview, and receives a refusal — that is a genuinely hard moment and we treat it as such.

We acknowledge it with empathy.
We immediately provide the appropriate next steps — 221(g) response guidance, attorney referral, practical options.
We do not disappear.

But we also do not accept liability for consular decisions. We never claimed to replace a lawyer. We never claimed to guarantee an outcome. We prepared them as thoroughly as a preparation tool can. Consular decisions involve factors — officer discretion, geopolitical context, personal presentation — that no software product can control or predict.

The disclaimer the user accepted at the start of their application covers this. But more importantly — we said it plainly, in plain language, before they ever paid. That is what makes it defensible: not the fine print, but the honesty.

Before shipping any feature, any screen, any word of copy — ask this:

## Platform Boundaries — What e2go Is and Is Not

e2go is a visa application preparation platform.
Our boundary is the documentation process.

What we do:
- Guide applicants through building a complete consulate-ready E-2 application package
- Educate on what consulates look for
- Connect applicants with brokers, franchisors, and attorneys as a convenience

What we never do:
- Recommend a specific business or franchise
- Tell an applicant their business qualifies
- Tell an applicant their business does not qualify
- Provide investment advice
- Provide legal advice

Approved framing:
Never: "this business qualifies for E-2"
Always: "here is what the consulate looks for in this business category"

Never: "this business does not qualify"
Always: "this category faces additional scrutiny — here is what that means for your application"

## Experience Is a Signal Never a Filter

When recommending franchise categories, experience in an industry boosts a recommendation — it never removes an option.
Many E-2 franchises are management businesses. The franchisee manages. Staff delivers.
Never say: "you need experience in X to qualify"
Always say: "your background in X strengthens the application narrative for this category"

## Franchise Nudge — Approved Framing

When a user has not decided on a business type, we educate on why franchises tend to produce stronger E-2 applications. This is factually accurate and genuinely in the applicant's interest.

Approved framing:
"Applicants with an established franchise tend to have stronger applications — the business model is proven, the financials are documented, and many franchises have processed E-2 visas before.
